🧲 Electrodynamics — Worked Examples & Practice

Grade 12 IEB Physics | Term 3

📌 Key Concepts

DC Motor: Electrical → Kinetic energy. Uses split-ring commutator to reverse current every ½ turn.
AC Generator: Kinetic → Electrical energy. Uses slip rings for continuous connection.
Fleming's LHR: Forefinger = Field, Middle finger = Current, Thumb = Force (Motion). For MOTORS.
Fleming's RHR: Forefinger = Field, Thumb = Motion, Middle finger = Induced current. For GENERATORS.
RMS Values: Vrms = Vmax/√2, Irms = Imax/√2, Pavg = Vrms × Irms
Force on conductor: F = BIL (conductor perpendicular to magnetic field)

Worked Example 1: DC Motor

A DC motor has a coil of 200 turns, each of area 0.05 m², placed in a field of 0.4 T. The coil carries 3 A.

1.1 State the energy conversion in a DC motor. (2)

1.2 When is the coil most effective (maximum torque)? Explain. (3)

1.3 State Fleming's Left Hand Rule. (2)

1.4 What is the purpose of the split-ring commutator? (3)

1.1 Electrical energy → Kinetic (mechanical) energy ✓

1.2 When the coil is parallel to the magnetic field — the perpendicular distance between forces is greatest, giving maximum torque. ✓

1.3 Thumb, forefinger, middle finger mutually perpendicular: Forefinger = Field, Middle = Current, Thumb = Force/Motion. ✓

1.4 Reverses current direction every half-turn so the coil keeps rotating in the same direction. Without it, the coil would oscillate and stop. ✓

Worked Example 2: AC Generator

Voltage graph: peak = 20 V, oscillates between +20 V and -20 V (sine wave)

2.1 What type of current is produced? (1)

2.2 Determine the peak voltage. (2)

2.3 Calculate the RMS voltage. (3)

2.4 Connected to a 10 Ω resistor. Calculate RMS current. (3)

2.5 Calculate average power. (3)

2.6 Explain the role of slip rings. (3)

2.1 Alternating current (AC) ✓

2.2 Vmax = 20 V ✓

2.3 Vrms = 20/√2 = 14.14 V ✓

2.4 Irms = 14.14/10 = 1.414 A ✓

2.5 Pavg = Vrms × Irms = 14.14 × 1.414 = 20 W ✓

2.6 Slip rings provide continuous electrical connection between rotating coil and external circuit — they do NOT reverse the current, allowing the external current to alternate. ✓

📝 Practice Activity 1: DC Motor

P1.1 Draw a labelled diagram of a simple DC motor showing: coil, magnetic poles, split-ring commutator, brushes, battery. (6)

P1.2 Describe what happens when current is switched on. (3)

P1.3 Why is the split-ring commutator essential? (4)

P1.4 State TWO ways to increase motor speed. (2)

P1.1 Diagram must show: N/S poles, rectangular coil, split-ring (2 half-rings), carbon brushes, battery, current direction arrows. ✓

P1.2 Current flows through coil → forces on coil sides in opposite directions (Fleming's LHR) → turning effect (torque) → coil rotates. ✓

P1.3 Reverses current direction every half-turn so forces always rotate coil the same way. Without it, coil would stop at vertical position. ✓

P1.4 1) Increase current. 2) Increase magnetic field strength (stronger magnets). 3) More turns on coil. ✓ (any two)

P2.5 Difference between slip rings and split-ring commutator. (4)

P2.1 Vrms = 100/√2 = 70.71 V ✓

P2.2 Irms = 70.71/25 = 2.83 A ✓

P2.3 Imax = Vmax/R = 100/25 = 4 A ✓

P2.4 1) Increase number of turns on coil. 2) Rotate coil faster. 3) Use stronger magnets. ✓ (any two)
