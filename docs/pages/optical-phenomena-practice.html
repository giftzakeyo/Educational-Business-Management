📌 Key Constants & Formulas

h = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s c = 3 × 10⁸ m·s⁻¹ e = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C mₑ = 9.11 × 10⁻³¹ kg 1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J
E = hf — Photon energy from frequency
E = hc/λ — Photon energy from wavelength
W₀ = hf₀ — Work function at threshold
Ek = hf - W₀ — Einstein's photoelectric equation
Ek = ½mv² — Max kinetic energy from velocity

Worked Example 1: Photon Energy

A light source emits radiation with wavelength 500 nm.

1.1 Convert 500 nm to metres. (1)

1.2 Calculate the energy of one photon. (3)

1.3 Express your answer in eV. (2)

1.4 What type of EM radiation is this? (1)

1.1 500 nm = 500 × 10⁻⁹ = 5.0 × 10⁻⁷ m ✓

1.2 E = hc/λ = (6.63×10⁻³⁴ × 3×10⁸)/(5.0×10⁻⁷) = 3.98 × 10⁻¹⁹ J ✓

1.3 E = 3.98×10⁻¹⁹/1.6×10⁻¹⁹ = 2.49 eV ✓

1.4 Visible light (blue-green, ~500 nm) ✓

Worked Example 2: Photoelectric Effect

A metal has work function 3.2 × 10⁻¹⁹ J. Light of frequency 1.2 × 10¹⁵ Hz is incident.

2.1 Define "work function". (2)

2.2 Calculate the threshold frequency. (3)

2.3 Will electrons be ejected? Justify. (4)

2.4 Calculate max kinetic energy of ejected electrons. (3)

2.5 Calculate the max speed of ejected electrons. (3)

2.1 Minimum energy required to eject an electron from the metal surface. ✓

2.2 f₀ = W₀/h = 3.2×10⁻¹⁹/6.63×10⁻³⁴ = 4.83 × 10¹⁴ Hz ✓

2.3 Incident f = 1.2×10¹⁵ Hz > f₀ = 4.83×10¹⁴ Hz → YES, electrons ejected. (E_photon = 7.96×10⁻¹⁹ J > W₀ = 3.2×10⁻¹⁹ J) ✓

2.4 Ek = hf - W₀ = 7.96×10⁻¹⁹ - 3.2×10⁻¹⁹ = 4.76 × 10⁻¹⁹ J ✓

2.5 v = √(2Ek/m) = √(2×4.76×10⁻¹⁹/9.11×10⁻³¹) = 1.02 × 10⁶ m·s⁻¹ ✓

📝 Practice Activity 1: Photon Calculations

P1.1 Energy of blue light photon (f = 6.5 × 10¹⁴ Hz). (3)

P1.2 Photon energy 4.5 × 10⁻¹⁹ J. Calculate wavelength in nm. (4)

P1.3 Which has more energy: red (700 nm) or violet (400 nm)? Show calculations. (4)

P1.4 Number of photons per second from a 60 W green light source (λ = 550 nm). (5)

P1.1 E = hf = (6.63×10⁻³⁴)(6.5×10¹⁴) = 4.31 × 10⁻¹⁹ J ✓

P1.2 λ = hc/E = (6.63×10⁻³⁴ × 3×10⁸)/(4.5×10⁻¹⁹) = 4.42×10⁻⁷ m = 442 nm ✓

P1.3 Red: E = (6.63×10⁻³⁴ × 3×10⁸)/(700×10⁻⁹) = 2.84×10⁻¹⁹ J. Violet: E = 4.97×10⁻¹⁹ J. Violet has MORE energy (shorter λ) ✓

P1.4 E/photon = hc/λ = 3.62×10⁻¹⁹ J. Number = 60/3.62×10⁻¹⁹ = 1.66 × 10²⁰ photons/s ✓

📝 Practice Activity 2: Photoelectric Effect

Light of frequency 8.0 × 10¹⁴ Hz on sodium (work function = 2.28 eV).

P2.1 Convert work function to joules. (2)

P2.2 Calculate threshold frequency. (3)

P2.3 Will emission occur? Show. (3)

P2.4 Calculate max KE in J and eV. (4)

P2.5 Effect of doubling intensity on: KE of electrons? Number of electrons? (4)

P2.1 W₀ = 2.28 × 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ = 3.65 × 10⁻¹⁹ J ✓

P2.2 f₀ = W₀/h = 3.65×10⁻¹⁹/6.63×10⁻³⁴ = 5.51 × 10¹⁴ Hz ✓

P2.3 8.0×10¹⁴ > 5.51×10¹⁴ → YES ✓

P2.4 Ek = (6.63×10⁻³⁴ × 8.0×10¹⁴) - 3.65×10⁻¹⁹ = 5.30×10⁻¹⁹ - 3.65×10⁻¹⁹ = 1.65×10⁻¹⁹ J = 1.03 eV ✓

P2.5 KE unchanged (depends on frequency). Number of electrons increases (more photons). ✓
