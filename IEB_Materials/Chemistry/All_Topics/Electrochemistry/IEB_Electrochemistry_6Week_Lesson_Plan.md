# IEB Physical Sciences: 6-Week Electrochemistry Lesson Plan (Grade 12)

## **Complete Teaching Schedule with Daily Activities, Assessments, and Resources**

---

## **Week 1: Redox Reactions & Galvanic Cells**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Define oxidation and reduction (OIL RIG)
- Identify oxidizing and reducing agents
- Understand galvanic cell components and operation
- Calculate standard cell potentials (E°cell)

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 1: Redox Basics**
- **Starter:** "What is rust? Why does it form?" discussion
- **Content:** Oxidation and reduction definitions
- **Memory tricks:** OIL RIG, LEO says GER
- **Activity:** Identifying redox in everyday reactions
- **Demo:** Iron wool burning (oxidation)
- **Homework:** Find 3 redox reactions at home

#### **Day 2: Oxidation Numbers**
- **Starter:** How to track electron transfer?
- **Content:** Oxidation number rules and calculations
- **Activity:** Determining oxidation numbers in compounds
- **Practice:** Various compound examples
- **Homework:** Oxidation number worksheet

#### **Day 3: Oxidizing & Reducing Agents**
- **Starter:** What causes oxidation/reduction?
- **Content:** Oxidizing agent (gets reduced), reducing agent (gets oxidized)
- **Activity:** Identifying agents in redox reactions
- **Practice:** Writing half-reactions
- **Assessment:** Redox basics quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Oxidizing/reducing agents worksheet

#### **Day 4: Galvanic Cells Introduction**
- **Starter:** How do batteries work?
- **Content:** Galvanic cell components and operation
- **Electrodes:** Anode (oxidation), cathode (reduction)
- **Demo:** Lemon/potato battery
- **Activity:** Labeling galvanic cell diagrams
- **Homework:** Galvanic cell components

#### **Day 5: Standard Electrode Potentials**
- **Starter:** Why do some metals react more vigorously?
- **Content:** E° values and their meaning
- **Table:** Common standard reduction potentials
- **Calculation:** E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode
- **Activity:** Predicting spontaneity from E° values
- **Homework:** E°cell calculations

---

## **Week 2: Cell Potentials & Nernst Equation**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Write cell notation correctly
- Calculate cell potentials under standard conditions
- Apply Nernst equation for non-standard conditions
- Understand concentration cells

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 6: Cell Notation**
- **Starter:** How to represent cells concisely?
- **Content:** Cell notation rules and conventions
- **Examples:** Various cell notations
- **Activity:** Writing notation for given cells
- **Practice:** Converting between diagrams and notation
- **Homework:** Cell notation worksheet

#### **Day 7: Standard Cell Potential Calculations**
- **Starter:** Predicting voltage from E° values
- **Content:** Systematic calculation method
- **Rules:** Identify cathode/anode, use formula
- **Activity:** Calculation practice with worked examples
- **Differentiation:** Tiered worksheets
- **Homework:** Standard potential calculations

#### **Day 8: Nernst Equation Introduction**
- **Starter:** Does concentration affect voltage?
- **Content:** Nernst equation derivation and meaning
- **Formula:** E = E° - (0.059/n)logQ at 25°C
- **Activity:** Simple Nernst calculations
- **Assessment:** Cell potential quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Nernst equation basics

#### **Day 9: Advanced Nernst Applications**
- **Starter:** Real batteries don't always have 1M solutions
- **Content:** Calculating E for given concentrations
- **Q calculation:** Reaction quotient for cell
- **Activity:** Various concentration scenarios
- **Practice:** Complex Nernst problems
- **Homework:** Advanced Nernst calculations

#### **Day 10: Concentration Cells**
- **Starter:** Can same electrodes produce voltage?
- **Content:** Concentration cell principles
- **Calculation:** E° = 0, voltage from concentration difference
- **Activity:** Concentration cell calculations
- **Assessment:** Weekly test (20 marks)
- **Reflection:** "Which calculation was most challenging?"

---

## **Week 3: Electrolysis & Faraday's Laws**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish between galvanic and electrolytic cells
- Understand electrolysis principles and applications
- Apply Faraday's laws to electrolysis calculations
- Perform electroplating calculations

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 11: Electrolysis Introduction**
- **Starter:** Can we force non-spontaneous reactions?
- **Content:** Electrolysis vs galvanic cells comparison
- **Electrode signs:** PANIC (Positive Anode, Negative Is Cathode)
- **Demo:** Electrolysis of water
- **Activity:** Comparing galvanic and electrolytic cells
- **Homework:** Electrolysis basics worksheet

#### **Day 12: Electrolysis Products**
- **Starter:** What forms at each electrode?
- **Content:** Predicting electrolysis products
- **Factors:** Electrode material, concentration, overpotential
- **Activity:** Predicting products for various electrolytes
- **Practice:** Different electrolysis scenarios
- **Homework:** Electrolysis products worksheet

#### **Day 13: Faraday's First Law**
- **Starter:** How much metal deposits during electroplating?
- **Content:** m ∝ Q relationship
- **Formula:** m = (Q × M)/(n × F)
- **Activity:** Simple Faraday calculations
- **Practice:** Various metal deposition problems
- **Assessment:** Electrolysis quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Faraday's first law problems

#### **Day 14: Faraday's Second Law & Applications**
- **Starter:** Same charge deposits different masses
- **Content:** Equivalent weights and Faraday's second law
- **Applications:** Electroplating, metal refining
- **Activity:** Comparing deposition of different metals
- **Practice:** Complex electrolysis problems
- **Homework:** Faraday's laws applications

#### **Day 15: Practical Session 1**
- **Title:** "Electroplating Copper"
- **Experiment:** Copper plating on iron object
- **Measurement:** Mass change, current, time
- **Calculation:** Faraday's law verification
- **Skills:** Electroplating setup, measurement, calculation
- **Assessment:** Lab report (20 marks)
- **Extension:** Comparing experimental vs theoretical

---

## **Week 4: Corrosion & Prevention**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Explain corrosion mechanisms (especially rusting)
- Identify factors affecting corrosion rates
- Understand corrosion prevention methods
- Apply electrochemical principles to corrosion

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 16: Corrosion Introduction**
- **Starter:** Why do metals corrode?
- **Content:** Corrosion as electrochemical process
- **Rusting mechanism:** Anode and cathode reactions
- **Activity:** Identifying corrosion in everyday life
- **Homework:** Corrosion observations

#### **Day 17: Rusting of Iron**
- **Starter:** Detailed rusting process
- **Content:** 4Fe + 3O₂ + 6H₂O → 4Fe(OH)₃
- **Electrochemical explanation:** Anode and cathode sites
- **Activity:** Writing rusting half-reactions
- **Practice:** Rusting mechanism explanations
- **Homework:** Rusting worksheet

#### **Day 18: Factors Affecting Corrosion**
- **Starter:** Why does salt accelerate rusting?
- **Content:** Moisture, oxygen, acids, salts, scratches
- **Demo:** Iron nails in different conditions
- **Activity:** Analyzing corrosion rate factors
- **Assessment:** Corrosion quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Corrosion factors analysis

#### **Day 19: Corrosion Prevention Methods**
- **Starter:** How do we protect metals?
- **Content:** Barrier methods, sacrificial protection, cathodic protection, alloying
- **Examples:** Painting, galvanizing, stainless steel
- **Activity:** Matching prevention methods to situations
- **Discussion:** Economic and practical considerations
- **Homework:** Corrosion prevention research

#### **Day 20: Practical Session 2**
- **Title:** "Investigating Corrosion"
- **Experiment:** Iron nails in different conditions
- **Conditions:** Dry, water, salt water, acid, with Zn
- **Observation:** Rust formation rates
- **Skills:** Experimental setup, observation, analysis
- **Assessment:** Lab report (20 marks)
- **Extension:** Quantitative rust measurement

---

## **Week 5: Batteries & Industrial Applications**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand different battery types and chemistries
- Compare battery performance characteristics
- Explain industrial electrochemical processes
- Apply electrochemistry to real-world technologies

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 21: Primary Batteries**
- **Starter:** How do disposable batteries work?
- **Content:** Dry cell and alkaline battery chemistry
- **Reactions:** Anode and cathode half-reactions
- **Activity:** Analyzing battery labels and specifications
- **Homework:** Battery chemistry research

#### **Day 22: Secondary Batteries**
- **Starter:** How do rechargeable batteries work?
- **Content:** Lead-acid and lithium-ion batteries
- **Charging/discharging:** Reversible reactions
- **Activity:** Comparing battery types
- **Practice:** Battery reaction writing
- **Assessment:** Batteries quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Battery comparison chart

#### **Day 23: Fuel Cells**
- **Starter:** What are fuel cells?
- **Content:** Hydrogen fuel cell operation
- **Advantages/disadvantages:** Clean but challenging
- **Activity:** Fuel cell diagram analysis
- **Discussion:** Future of fuel cell technology
- **Homework:** Fuel cell research

#### **Day 24: Industrial Electrochemistry**
- **Starter:** Electrochemistry in industry
- **Content:** Electrorefining, chlor-alkali process, aluminum production
- **South African focus:** Mining applications
- **Activity:** Industrial process analysis
- **Discussion:** Environmental and economic aspects
- **Homework:** Industrial application case study

#### **Day 25: Environmental Applications**
- **Starter:** Electrochemistry and environment
- **Content:** Water treatment, pollution control, energy storage
- **Activity:** Environmental problem-solving
- **Discussion:** Sustainable electrochemical technologies
- **Homework:** Environmental impact assessment

---

## **Week 6: Integration & Exam Preparation**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Solve complex integrated electrochemistry problems
- Apply multiple concepts to real scenarios
- Prepare for IEB examinations
- Develop exam technique for electrochemistry questions

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 26: Complex Problem Solving**
- **Starter:** Multi-concept electrochemistry problem
- **Content:** Integrating redox, potentials, Nernst, Faraday
- **Strategy:** Step-by-step problem decomposition
- **Activity:** Worked complex examples
- **Practice:** Independent problem solving
- **Homework:** Complex electrochemistry problems

#### **Day 27: Exam Technique Workshop**
- **Starter:** Common IEB electrochemistry question types
- **Content:** Reading questions, showing working, time management
- **Activity:** Marking sample answers
- **Practice:** Under exam conditions
- **Homework:** Past paper section

#### **Day 28: Revision Stations**
- **Setup:** 5 stations (15 min each)
  1. Redox reactions and agents
  2. Cell potential calculations
  3. Nernst equation applications
  4. Faraday's laws calculations
  5. Applications (corrosion, batteries, etc.)
- **Activity:** Rotate through stations
- **Review:** Key concepts and formulas
- **Homework:** Comprehensive revision

#### **Day 29: Past Paper Practice**
- **Paper:** IEB 2023 Paper 2 (electrochemistry section)
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Marks:** 25
- **Activity:** Under exam conditions
- **After:** Self-mark using memorandum
- **Homework:** Error correction and improvement

#### **Day 30: Final Assessment**
- **Duration:** 1 hour
- **Marks:** 50
- **Format:** IEB-style (multiple choice, calculations, explanations, applications)
- **Content:** All electrochemistry topics
- **After test:** Feedback and improvement planning

## **Differentiation Strategies:**

### **For Lower-Achieving Students:**
- Simplified worksheets with worked examples
- More visual aids and hands-on demonstrations
- Focus on qualitative understanding first
- Peer tutoring opportunities
- Formula sheets with explanations

### **For Higher-Achieving Students:**
- Complex Nernst and Faraday problems
- Research on advanced battery technology
- Design of electrochemical experiments
- Participation in science competitions
- Peer teaching responsibilities

### **For English Additional Language (EAL) Students:**
- Bilingual vocabulary lists
- Sentence frames for explanations
- Visual vocabulary cards
- Partner work with language support
- Additional time for reading tasks

## **Final Thoughts:**

Electrochemistry provides a crucial bridge between chemical reactions and electrical energy. By connecting redox concepts with practical applications in energy storage, corrosion prevention, and industrial processes, students develop both conceptual understanding and practical problem-solving skills.

The key to successful IEB teaching is balancing theoretical understanding with practical applications, particularly those relevant to South African industry and emerging energy technologies.

**Remember:** The goal is not just to calculate cell potentials, but to understand how electrochemical principles power our technology, protect our infrastructure, and shape our energy future.
