# IEB Physical Sciences: 6-Week Chemical Equilibrium Lesson Plan (Grade 12)

## **Complete Teaching Schedule with Daily Activities, Assessments, and Resources**

---

## **Week 1: Dynamic Equilibrium & Kc Introduction**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Define dynamic equilibrium and its characteristics
- Distinguish between reversible and irreversible reactions
- Write equilibrium expressions (Kc)
- Calculate Kc from equilibrium concentrations

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 1: Introduction to Equilibrium**
- **Starter:** "What does equilibrium mean?" (See-saw analogy)
- **Content:** Dynamic vs static equilibrium
- **Characteristics:** Rates equal, concentrations constant, microscopic activity
- **Demo:** Cobalt chloride equilibrium (pink ⇌ blue)
- **Activity:** Identifying equilibrium systems
- **Homework:** Find 3 equilibrium examples at home

#### **Day 2: Reversible Reactions**
- **Starter:** One-way vs two-way reactions
- **Content:** Reversible (⇌) vs irreversible (→) reactions
- **Examples:** Physical (melting/freezing) and chemical (esterification)
- **Activity:** Classifying reactions as reversible/irreversible
- **Homework:** Reversible reactions worksheet

#### **Day 3: Equilibrium Constant Kc**
- **Starter:** What does "constant" mean in chemistry?
- **Content:** Kc definition and expression
- **Formula:** Kc = [products]ᵖ/[reactants]ʳ
- **Activity:** Writing Kc expressions for given reactions
- **Practice:** Simple expression writing
- **Homework:** Kc expression worksheet

#### **Day 4: Calculating Kc from Data**
- **Starter:** Given concentrations, find ratio
- **Content:** Substituting values into Kc expression
- **Calculation:** Step-by-step examples
- **Activity:** Calculation practice with worked examples
- **Differentiation:** Tiered worksheets
- **Homework:** Kc calculation worksheet 1

#### **Day 5: Kc Interpretation**
- **Starter:** What does Kc value tell us?
- **Content:** Kc > 1 (products favored), Kc < 1 (reactants favored)
- **Activity:** Predicting equilibrium position from Kc values
- **Assessment:** Kc concepts quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Kc interpretation exercises

---

## **Week 2: ICE Tables & Equilibrium Calculations**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Set up and use ICE tables
- Calculate equilibrium concentrations from initial conditions
- Solve equilibrium problems using quadratic equations
- Apply approximation method for small Kc values

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 6: ICE Tables Introduction**
- **Starter:** Organizing information (I = Initial, C = Change, E = Equilibrium)
- **Content:** ICE table structure and purpose
- **Demo:** Filling ICE table for simple reaction
- **Activity:** ICE table practice with guided examples
- **Homework:** Basic ICE table problems

#### **Day 7: Simple ICE Table Calculations**
- **Starter:** Given equilibrium concentration, find others
- **Content:** Solving for x in simple cases
- **Activity:** Worked examples then independent practice
- **Practice:** Problems where x is easily found
- **Homework:** ICE table worksheet 1

#### **Day 8: Quadratic Equation Solutions**
- **Starter:** When ICE tables give quadratic equations
- **Content:** Setting up and solving quadratics
- **Math review:** Quadratic formula application
- **Activity:** Step-by-step quadratic solutions
- **Assessment:** Quadratic solving quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Quadratic equilibrium problems

#### **Day 9: Approximation Method**
- **Starter:** When Kc is very small (<10⁻³)
- **Content:** Approximation justification and method
- **Activity:** Comparing exact vs approximate solutions
- **Practice:** Problems suitable for approximation
- **Homework:** Approximation method worksheet

#### **Day 10: Complex Equilibrium Problems**
- **Starter:** Putting it all together
- **Content:** Multi-step equilibrium problems
- **Strategy:** Systematic problem-solving approach
- **Activity:** Complex problem workshop
- **Assessment:** Weekly test (20 marks)
- **Reflection:** "Which calculation method was hardest?"

---

## **Week 3: Le Chatelier's Principle**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- State and explain Le Chatelier's principle
- Predict equilibrium shifts for concentration changes
- Predict equilibrium shifts for pressure changes (gases)
- Predict equilibrium shifts for temperature changes

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 11: Le Chatelier Introduction**
- **Starter:** "If you push it, it pushes back"
- **Content:** Le Chatelier's principle statement
- **Analogy:** Crowded room disturbance
- **Activity:** Predicting shifts in simple systems
- **Homework:** Le Chatelier basics worksheet

#### **Day 12: Concentration Changes**
- **Starter:** Adding/removing reactants/products
- **Content:** Shift to use up added substance or make more removed substance
- **Rules:** Add reactant → shift right, Remove product → shift right, etc.
- **Activity:** Concentration change predictions
- **Practice:** Various scenarios
- **Homework:** Concentration change problems

#### **Day 13: Pressure Changes (Gases)**
- **Starter:** Why pressure affects gases only?
- **Content:** Shift to side with fewer gas molecules
- **Calculation:** Counting gas molecules (Δn)
- **Demo:** Nitrogen dioxide equilibrium (2NO₂ ⇌ N₂O₄)
- **Activity:** Pressure change predictions
- **Assessment:** Pressure effects quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Pressure change worksheet

#### **Day 14: Temperature Changes**
- **Starter:** Need to know if reaction is exo/endo
- **Content:** Shift in endothermic direction when temperature increased
- **Memory aid:** "Heat likes endothermic"
- **Activity:** Temperature change predictions
- **Practice:** Given ΔH, predict temperature effects
- **Homework:** Temperature effects worksheet

#### **Day 15: Catalyst Effects & Integration**
- **Starter:** Do catalysts affect equilibrium position?
- **Content:** Catalysts speed up both directions equally
- **Activity:** Integrating all factors
- **Review:** All Le Chatelier applications
- **Assessment:** Le Chatelier test (15 marks)
- **Homework:** Comprehensive Le Chatelier problems

---

## **Week 4: Reaction Quotient Q & Industrial Applications**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Calculate reaction quotient Q
- Compare Q and K to predict shift direction
- Apply equilibrium principles to industrial processes
- Analyze optimization of industrial reactions

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 16: Reaction Quotient Q**
- **Starter:** Kc for equilibrium, Q for any conditions
- **Content:** Q formula (same as Kc), comparison with K
- **Rules:** Q < K → shift right, Q > K → shift left, Q = K → equilibrium
- **Activity:** Q calculation and comparison practice
- **Homework:** Reaction quotient worksheet

#### **Day 17: Q Applications**
- **Starter:** Predicting shift direction from initial conditions
- **Content:** Using Q to predict if reaction will proceed
- **Activity:** Various Q application problems
- **Practice:** Real-world scenarios
- **Assessment:** Q calculation quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Q application problems

#### **Day 18: Haber Process Case Study**
- **Starter:** How is ammonia made industrially?
- **Content:** N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ (ΔH = -92 kJ/mol)
- **Optimization:** High pressure, moderate temperature, catalyst, removal of NH₃
- **Activity:** Analyzing Haber process conditions
- **Discussion:** Economic vs yield considerations
- **Homework:** Haber process analysis

#### **Day 19: Contact Process Case Study**
- **Starter:** Sulfuric acid production
- **Content:** 2SO₂ + O₂ ⇌ 2SO₃ (ΔH = -197 kJ/mol)
- **Optimization:** Excess O₂, moderate temperature, catalyst, removal of SO₃
- **Activity:** Comparing Haber and Contact processes
- **Assessment:** Industrial process quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Contact process worksheet

#### **Day 20: Other Industrial Applications**
- **Starter:** Equilibrium in other industries
- **Content:** Esterification, hydration/dehydration, etc.
- **South African focus:** SASOL processes, mining applications
- **Activity:** Research and present one industrial application
- **Homework:** Industrial application research

## **Week 5: Biological & Environmental Equilibria**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Apply equilibrium concepts to biological systems
- Understand environmental equilibrium systems
- Explain buffer action using equilibrium principles
- Analyze real-world equilibrium phenomena

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 21: Biological Equilibria Introduction**
- **Starter:** Equilibrium in your body?
- **Content:** Hemoglobin-O₂, blood buffers, enzyme reactions
- **Activity:** Identifying biological equilibria
- **Practice:** Biological equilibrium examples
- **Homework:** Biological equilibria worksheet

#### **Day 22: Hemoglobin-Oxygen Equilibrium**
- **Starter:** How does blood carry oxygen?
- **Content:** Hb + 4O₂ ⇌ Hb(O₂)₄
- **Factors:** Partial pressure, pH, temperature
- **Application:** High altitude, exercise, CO poisoning
- **Activity:** Analyzing hemoglobin equilibrium
- **Homework:** Hemoglobin problems

#### **Day 23: Blood Buffer System**
- **Starter:** How does blood maintain pH ~7.4?
- **Content:** H₂CO₃ ⇌ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻ buffer system
- **Action:** Adding acid/base shifts equilibrium
- **Activity:** Buffer action explanations
- **Assessment:** Biological equilibrium quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Buffer system analysis

#### **Day 24: Environmental Equilibria**
- **Starter:** Equilibrium in environment?
- **Content:** Ocean carbonate system, acid rain, ozone layer
- **Application:** Climate change, pollution effects
- **Activity:** Environmental equilibrium case studies
- **Discussion:** Human impact on natural equilibria
- **Homework:** Environmental equilibrium research

#### **Day 25: Practical Session**
- **Title:** "Investigating Cobalt Chloride Equilibrium"
- **System:** CoCl₄²⁻(blue) + 6H₂O ⇌ Co(H₂O)₆²⁺(pink) + 4Cl⁻
- **Disturbances:** Add water, add HCl, heat, cool
- **Skills:** Observation, Le Chatelier application
- **Assessment:** Lab report (20 marks)
- **Extension:** Quantitative measurements

## **Final Thoughts:**

Chemical equilibrium provides a crucial understanding of how chemical systems behave under different conditions. By connecting microscopic particle behavior with macroscopic observations and industrial applications, students develop both conceptual understanding and practical problem-solving skills.

The key to successful IEB teaching is balancing theoretical understanding with practical applications, particularly those relevant to South African industry, biology, and environment.

**Remember:** The goal is not just to calculate Kc values, but to understand how equilibrium principles govern chemical systems in industry, biology, and the environment, and how we can manipulate these systems for beneficial purposes.
