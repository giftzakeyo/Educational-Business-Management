# IEB Physical Sciences: 5-Week Energy and Chemical Change Lesson Plan (Grade 12)

## **Complete Teaching Schedule with Daily Activities, Assessments, and Resources**

---

## **Week 1: Foundation & Calorimetry**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish between exothermic and endothermic processes
- Understand enthalpy change (ΔH) and sign conventions
- Perform calorimetry calculations using q = mcΔT
- Conduct simple calorimetry experiments

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 1: Introduction to Energy Changes**
- **Starter:** "Hot or Cold?" picture sort (burning, melting, etc.)
- **Content:** Exothermic vs endothermic definitions
- **Memory trick:** EXOthermic = EXit heat, ENdothermic = ENter heat
- **Demo:** Hand warmers vs cold packs
- **Activity:** Classifying everyday processes
- **Homework:** Find 3 exo and 3 endo examples at home

#### **Day 2: Enthalpy Change (ΔH)**
- **Starter:** What does ΔH mean? (Brainstorm)
- **Content:** ΔH definition, units (kJ/mol), sign conventions
- **Visual:** Energy profile diagrams
- **Activity:** Drawing and interpreting ΔH diagrams
- **Practice:** Identifying ΔH sign from descriptions
- **Homework:** ΔH worksheet

#### **Day 3: Calorimetry Principles**
- **Starter:** How do we measure heat changes?
- **Content:** Calorimetry basics, q = mcΔT formula
- **Values:** Specific heat capacities (water = 4.18 J/g·°C)
- **Activity:** Simple calculation practice
- **Differentiation:** Tiered worksheets (basic → advanced)
- **Homework:** Calorimetry calculation worksheet 1

#### **Day 4: Practical Session 1**
- **Title:** "Neutralization Calorimetry"
- **Reaction:** HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
- **Measurement:** Temperature change
- **Calculation:** q then ΔH
- **Skills:** Temperature measurement, calculation, error analysis
- **Assessment:** Data table and calculation (15 marks)

#### **Day 5: Advanced Calorimetry**
- **Starter:** Common errors in calorimetry
- **Content:** Improving accuracy, error sources
- **Activity:** Error analysis of yesterday's results
- **Practice:** Complex calorimetry problems
- **Assessment:** Calorimetry quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Error analysis questions

---

## **Week 2: Standard Enthalpy Changes**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand different standard enthalpy changes
- Calculate ΔH from experimental data
- Apply ΔH = q/n formula
- Interpret enthalpy data in context

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 6: Standard Conditions**
- **Starter:** What does "standard" mean in chemistry?
- **Content:** Standard states, conditions (25°C, 100 kPa)
- **Types:** ΔH°c, ΔH°f, ΔH°neut, ΔH°sol
- **Activity:** Matching definitions to types
- **Homework:** Standard enthalpy definitions

#### **Day 7: Combustion Enthalpy**
- **Starter:** Why do fuels release heat when burned?
- **Content:** ΔH°c definition and applications
- **Values:** Common fuels (methane, ethanol, etc.)
- **Activity:** Comparing fuel energy values
- **Practice:** Combustion calculations
- **Homework:** Fuel energy comparison

#### **Day 8: Formation Enthalpy**
- **Starter:** Elements in standard states have ΔH°f = 0
- **Content:** ΔH°f definition and uses
- **Application:** Calculating ΔH from ΔH°f values
- **Activity:** Using data tables
- **Assessment:** Formation enthalpy quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** ΔH°f calculations

#### **Day 9: Neutralization Enthalpy**
- **Starter:** Why do acid-base reactions get warm?
- **Content:** ΔH°neut, typical values
- **Factors:** Strong vs weak acids/bases
- **Practical:** Comparing different acid-base pairs
- **Analysis:** Results and explanations
- **Homework:** Neutralization problems

#### **Day 10: Integration & Applications**
- **Starter:** Real-world applications brainstorm
- **Activity:** Energy application research
- **Review:** All standard enthalpy types
- **Practice:** Integrated problem solving
- **Assessment:** Weekly test (20 marks)
- **Reflection:** "Which ΔH type was most challenging?"

---

## **Week 3: Hess's Law & Energy Cycles**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- State and explain Hess's Law
- Use Hess's Law to calculate unknown ΔH values
- Draw and interpret energy cycle diagrams
- Apply Hess's Law to complex problems

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 11: Hess's Law Introduction**
- **Starter:** Mountain climbing analogy
- **Content:** Hess's Law statement and meaning
- **Visual:** Energy pathway diagrams
- **Activity:** Simple Hess's Law examples
- **Homework:** Hess's Law explanation worksheet

#### **Day 12: Hess's Law Calculations Method 1**
- **Starter:** Adding chemical equations
- **Content:** Equation manipulation method
- **Rules:** Reverse equation = sign change, Multiply = multiply ΔH
- **Activity:** Step-by-step worked examples
- **Practice:** Calculation exercises
- **Homework:** Hess's Law calculation worksheet 1

#### **Day 13: Hess's Law Calculations Method 2**
- **Starter:** Energy cycle diagrams
- **Content:** Diagram method for Hess's Law
- **Visual:** Drawing and interpreting cycles
- **Activity:** Diagram practice
- **Practice:** Both methods comparison
- **Homework:** Energy cycle diagrams

#### **Day 14: Complex Hess's Law Problems**
- **Starter:** Three-equation problems
- **Content:** Advanced Hess's Law applications
- **Strategy:** Systematic approach
- **Activity:** Worked complex examples
- **Assessment:** Hess's Law test (15 marks)
- **Homework:** Complex problem set

#### **Day 15: Practical Session 2**
- **Title:** "Combustion Calorimetry"
- **Reaction:** Alcohol burning (ethanol/methanol)
- **Measurement:** Temperature rise of water
- **Calculation:** ΔH°c from experimental data
- **Skills:** Safe combustion, calorimeter use, calculation
- **Assessment:** Lab report (20 marks)
- **Extension:** Compare experimental vs literature values

---

## **Week 4: Bond Energies & Applications**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand bond breaking and forming energetics
- Calculate ΔH using bond energies
- Apply energy concepts to real situations
- Evaluate energy efficiency of processes

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 16: Bond Energy Concept**
- **Starter:** Why do some reactions release heat?
- **Content:** Bond breaking (endo) vs forming (exo)
- **Formula:** ΔH = Σ(broken) - Σ(formed)
- **Activity:** Simple bond energy calculations
- **Homework:** Bond energy basics worksheet

#### **Day 17: Bond Energy Values**
- **Starter:** Common bond energies memory game
- **Content:** Key values (C-H, C-C, O-H, etc.)
- **Application:** Using data tables
- **Activity:** Bond energy calculation practice
- **Practice:** Various reaction examples
- **Homework:** Bond energy calculations

#### **Day 18: Comparing Methods**
- **Starter:** Hess's Law vs bond energies vs calorimetry
- **Content:** Strengths and limitations of each method
- **Activity:** Solving same problem different ways
- **Analysis:** Which method is best for which situation?
- **Assessment:** Method comparison questions (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Method application exercises

#### **Day 19: Industrial Applications**
- **Starter:** Energy in South African industry
- **Content:** SASOL, mining, manufacturing
- **Case study:** Haber process energy considerations
- **Activity:** Energy efficiency analysis
- **Discussion:** Economic vs environmental factors
- **Homework:** Industry research project

#### **Day 20: Environmental Applications**
- **Starter:** Energy and the environment
- **Content:** Renewable vs non-renewable energy
- **Applications:** Cold packs, phase change materials
- **Activity:** Designing energy-efficient solutions
- **Assessment:** Application questions (15 marks)
- **Homework:** Environmental impact analysis

---

## **Week 5: Advanced Topics & Exam Preparation**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand Born-Haber cycles (qualitative)
- Introduce spontaneity and entropy concepts
- Prepare for IEB examinations
- Develop exam technique

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 21: Born-Haber Cycles**
- **Starter:** Lattice energy mystery
- **Content:** Born-Haber cycle concept (qualitative)
- **Visual:** Simplified cycle diagrams
- **Activity:** Interpreting given cycles
- **Homework:** Born-Haber cycle worksheet

#### **Day 22: Spontaneity & Entropy**
- **Starter:** Why do some reactions happen spontaneously?
- **Content:** Gibbs free energy (ΔG = ΔH - TΔS) introduction
- **Concept:** Entropy as disorder
- **Activity:** Predicting spontaneity from ΔH and ΔS
- **Homework:** Spontaneity exercises

#### **Day 23: Exam Technique Workshop**
- **Starter:** Common IEB exam mistakes in energy questions
- **Content:** Reading questions, showing working, time management
- **Activity:** Marking sample answers
- **Practice:** Under exam conditions
- **Homework:** Past paper section

#### **Day 24: Revision & Integration**
- **Starter:** "What are you still unsure about?"
- **Activity:** Revision stations (5 stations, 15 min each)
  1. Calorimetry calculations
  2. Hess's Law problems
  3. Bond energy calculations
  4. Diagram drawing and interpretation
  5. Peer teaching of weak areas
- **Review:** Key concepts and formulas
- **Homework:** Comprehensive revision

#### **Day 25: Final Assessment**
- **Duration:** 1 hour
- **Marks:** 50
- **Format:** IEB-style (multiple choice, calculations, explanations, diagrams)
- **Content:** All energy topics
- **After test:** Feedback and improvement planning

## **Resources Required:**

### **Essential Equipment:**
1. Calorimeters (polystyrene cups with lids)
2. Thermometers or temperature probes
3. Measuring cylinders and balances
4. Spirit burners or alcohol burners
5. Data loggers (optional but recommended)

### **Chemicals Needed:**
1. Hydrochloric acid and sodium hydroxide
2. Ethanol or methanol for combustion
3. Various salts for dissolution experiments
4. Commercial cold/hot packs for demonstration
5. Standard laboratory chemicals

### **Safety Considerations:**
- Eye protection always worn
- Acid/base handling procedures followed
- Combustion safety protocols
- Hot equipment handling procedures
- General laboratory safety rules enforced

### **Digital Resources:**
1. PhET energy simulations
2. Online Hess's Law calculators
3. Video demonstrations of experiments
4. IEB past papers and memoranda
5. Interactive energy diagram tools

## **Final Thoughts:**

Energy and chemical change provides a crucial bridge between observable phenomena and quantitative analysis. By connecting sensory experiences (hot/cold) with mathematical models, students develop both conceptual understanding and practical skills.

The key to successful IEB teaching is balancing concrete experiences with abstract thinking, while making connections to relevant South African energy contexts and challenges.

**Remember:** The goal is not just to calculate ΔH values, but to understand energy flow in chemical processes and how this knowledge impacts our world, economy, and environment.
