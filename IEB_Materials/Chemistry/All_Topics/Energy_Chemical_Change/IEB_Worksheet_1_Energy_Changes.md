### **Section B: Definitions (5 marks)**
1. **Exothermic:** Heat released to surroundings, ΔH negative (1)
2. **Enthalpy change:** Heat change at constant pressure, ΔH (1)
3. **Calorimetry:** Measurement of heat changes in reactions (1)
4. **Hess's Law:** Total enthalpy change independent of pathway (1)
5. **Activation energy:** Minimum energy needed for reaction to occur (1)

### **Section C: Calculations (10 marks)**
1. q = 250 × 4.18 × 60 = 62,700 J = 62.7 kJ (2)
2. ΔH = 1.2 ÷ 0.02 = -60 kJ/mol (negative as heat released) (2)
3. Broken: 436 + 243 = 679 kJ, Formed: 2 × 432 = 864 kJ, ΔH = 679 - 864 = -185 kJ/mol (2)
4. q = 50 × 4.18 × 10.5 = 2194.5 J = 2.1945 kJ, ΔH = 2.1945 ÷ 0.01 = -219.5 kJ/mol (2)
5. A → C = A → B + B → C = -40 + (-60) = -100 kJ (2)

### **Section D: Explanations (5 marks)**
1. More energy released forming bonds (CO₂, H₂O) than breaking bonds (wood, O₂) (1)
2. Measure temperature change, use q = mcΔT, divide by moles for ΔH (1)
3. If bonds formed stronger than bonds broken → exothermic, if weaker → endothermic (1)
4. Any two: Hand warmers, combustion engines, respiration, etc. (1)
5. Allows calculation of ΔH for reactions hard to measure directly (1)
