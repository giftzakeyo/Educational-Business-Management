### **Section B: Definitions (5 marks)**
1. **Reaction rate:** How fast reactants turn into products (1)
2. **Activation energy:** Minimum energy needed for reaction to occur (1)
3. **Catalyst:** Substance that increases rate without being used up (1)
4. **Collision theory:** Particles must collide with correct orientation and sufficient energy (1)
5. **Surface area:** Amount of exposed area of a substance (1)

### **Section C: Calculations (10 marks)**
1. Rate = 2.4 ÷ 60 = 0.04 g/s (2: formula + answer)
2. Rate = (0.80 - 0.20) ÷ 40 = 0.60 ÷ 40 = 0.015 mol/L·s (2)
3. Rate = (50 - 30) ÷ (40 - 20) = 20 ÷ 20 = 1.0 cm³/s (2)
4. Rate = 120 ÷ 60 = 2.0 cm³/s (2)
5. Time = 0.50 ÷ 0.025 = 20 seconds (2)

### **Section D: Explanations (5 marks)**
1. Powder has larger surface area, more particles exposed for collisions (1)
2. Provides alternative pathway with lower activation energy, regenerated (1)
3. Particles have more kinetic energy, move faster, collide more frequently with greater energy (1)
4. Any two: e.g., Platinum in car exhaust, enzymes in digestion (1)
5. Concentration decreases, fewer collisions between particles (1)
