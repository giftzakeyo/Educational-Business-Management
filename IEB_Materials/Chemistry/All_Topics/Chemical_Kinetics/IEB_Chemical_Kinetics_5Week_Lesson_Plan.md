# IEB Physical Sciences: 5-Week Chemical Kinetics Lesson Plan (Grade 12)

## **Complete Teaching Schedule with Daily Activities, Assessments, and Resources**

---

## **Week 1: Foundation & Rate Measurements**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Define reaction rate and understand what it measures
- Calculate average reaction rates from experimental data
- Understand different methods of measuring rates
- Interpret simple rate data

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 1: Introduction to Reaction Rates**
- **Starter:** "Fast vs Slow Reactions" picture sort
- **Content:** What is reaction rate? Why does it matter?
- **Activity:** Timing everyday reactions (Alka-Seltzer in water)
- **Demo:** Sodium thiosulfate clock reaction
- **Homework:** Find 3 examples of fast/slow reactions at home

#### **Day 2: Measuring Reaction Rates**
- **Starter:** How could we measure these rates? (Brainstorm)
- **Content:** Methods: mass loss, gas collection, color change, precipitation
- **Activity:** Designing measurement methods for different reactions
- **Practice:** Choosing appropriate measurement techniques
- **Homework:** Worksheet on measurement methods

#### **Day 3: Rate Calculations**
- **Starter:** Speed analogy (km/h → mol/L·s)
- **Content:** Rate = Δamount ÷ Δtime
- **Formula:** Average rate calculations
- **Activity:** Calculation practice with worked examples
- **Differentiation:** Tiered worksheets (basic → advanced)
- **Homework:** Calculation worksheet 1

#### **Day 4: Practical Session 1**
- **Title:** "Measuring Rate of Reaction"
- **Reaction:** Magnesium + hydrochloric acid
- **Measurement:** Gas collection over time
- **Skills:** Timing, volume measurement, data recording
- **Assessment:** Data table completion (10 marks)
- **Extension:** Calculate rates at different time intervals

#### **Day 5: Data Analysis & Graphs**
- **Starter:** Plotting data from yesterday's practical
- **Content:** Drawing and interpreting concentration-time graphs
- **Activity:** Graph plotting practice
- **Analysis:** What does the graph shape tell us?
- **Assessment:** Graph interpretation quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Complete graph analysis questions

---

## **Week 2: Collision Theory & Factors Affecting Rate**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand and explain collision theory
- Describe factors affecting reaction rates
- Explain why each factor affects the rate
- Apply knowledge to predict rate changes

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 6: Collision Theory Introduction**
- **Starter:** "Successful vs Unsuccessful collisions" animation
- **Content:** Three requirements for reaction
- **Analogy:** School dance/party analogy
- **Activity:** Role-play collision scenarios
- **Homework:** Collision theory explanation worksheet

#### **Day 7: Temperature Effect**
- **Starter:** Why does food cook faster when hotter?
- **Content:** Temperature and kinetic energy
- **Rule:** 10°C increase ≈ double the rate
- **Demo:** Two beakers at different temperatures
- **Activity:** Predicting temperature effects
- **Homework:** Temperature calculations

#### **Day 8: Concentration Effect**
- **Starter:** More crowded = more collisions (classroom analogy)
- **Content:** Concentration vs rate relationship
- **Practical:** Sodium thiosulfate with different concentrations
- **Analysis:** Results and explanation
- **Homework:** Concentration problems

#### **Day 9: Surface Area Effect**
- **Starter:** Sugar cube vs powdered sugar dissolving
- **Content:** Surface area explanation
- **Demo:** Marble chips vs powder with acid
- **Activity:** Calculating surface area ratios
- **Assessment:** Explanation questions (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Surface area applications

#### **Day 10: Factors Integration**
- **Starter:** "Which factor is most important?" debate
- **Activity:** Factor sorting game
- **Review:** All factors and their explanations
- **Practice:** Integrated problem solving
- **Assessment:** Weekly test (20 marks)
- **Reflection:** "Which factor was hardest to understand?"

---

## **Week 3: Activation Energy & Catalysis**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Define and explain activation energy
- Interpret energy profile diagrams
- Understand how catalysts work
- Distinguish between homogeneous and heterogeneous catalysis

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 11: Activation Energy Concept**
- **Starter:** "The hill you must climb" analogy
- **Content:** What is activation energy? Why does it matter?
- **Visual:** Energy profile diagrams
- **Activity:** Drawing and labeling energy profiles
- **Homework:** Energy profile worksheet

#### **Day 12: Catalysts Introduction**
- **Starter:** "Helpers that aren't used up" mystery
- **Content:** What are catalysts? How do they work?
- **Demo:** Hydrogen peroxide with manganese(IV) oxide
- **Activity:** Identifying catalysts in everyday life
- **Homework:** Catalyst research

#### **Day 13: How Catalysts Work**
- **Starter:** Comparing energy profiles with/without catalyst
- **Content:** Alternative pathway with lower Eₐ
- **Analogy:** Tunnel through a mountain vs climbing over
- **Activity:** Drawing catalyzed energy profiles
- **Practice:** Explaining catalyst action
- **Homework:** Explanation exercises

#### **Day 14: Types of Catalysts**
- **Starter:** Same phase vs different phase
- **Content:** Homogeneous vs heterogeneous
- **Examples:** Enzymes (homogeneous) vs platinum (heterogeneous)
- **Activity:** Classifying different catalysts
- **Assessment:** Catalyst classification quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Type comparison table

#### **Day 15: Practical Session 2**
- **Title:** "Investigating Catalysis"
- **Reaction:** Hydrogen peroxide decomposition
- **Catalysts:** Manganese(IV) oxide, liver, potato
- **Measurement:** Foam height or oxygen production
- **Skills:** Observation, comparison, explanation
- **Assessment:** Lab report (20 marks)
- **Extension:** Research on enzyme catalysis

---

## **Week 4: Rate Laws & Advanced Concepts**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand rate laws and rate constants
- Determine order of reaction from data
- Interpret rate-concentration graphs
- Solve advanced rate problems

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 16: Rate Laws Introduction**
- **Starter:** "Mathematical relationships" preview
- **Content:** What are rate laws? Rate = k[reactant]ⁿ
- **Simple examples:** Zero, first, second order
- **Activity:** Matching rate laws to descriptions
- **Homework:** Basic rate law problems

#### **Day 17: Determining Order**
- **Starter:** Data table analysis
- **Content:** How to determine order from data
- **Method:** Compare how rate changes with concentration
- **Activity:** Order determination practice
- **Differentiation:** Scaffolded worksheets
- **Homework:** Order determination problems

#### **Day 18: Rate Constants**
- **Starter:** What does "k" represent?
- **Content:** Rate constant meaning and units
- **Calculation:** Finding k from rate law
- **Activity:** Rate constant calculations
- **Practice:** Units for different orders
- **Homework:** Rate constant worksheet

#### **Day 19: Graph Interpretation**
- **Starter:** Different graph shapes for different orders
- **Content:** Zero, first, second order graphs
- **Activity:** Matching graphs to orders
- **Practice:** Interpreting given graphs
- **Assessment:** Graph interpretation test (15 marks)
- **Homework:** Graph drawing practice

#### **Day 20: Integrated Problem Solving**
- **Starter:** Complex problem example
- **Content:** Putting it all together
- **Strategy:** Step-by-step problem solving
- **Activity:** Worked examples then independent practice
- **Review:** Common mistakes and how to avoid them
- **Homework:** Comprehensive problem set

---

## **Week 5: Applications & Exam Preparation**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Apply kinetic principles to real-world situations
- Understand industrial applications
- Prepare for IEB examinations
- Develop exam technique

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 21: Industrial Applications**
- **Starter:** "Where is kinetics used in industry?"
- **Content:** Haber process, catalytic converters, food industry
- **South African focus:** SASOL, mining applications
- **Activity:** Research and present one application
- **Homework:** Application research project

#### **Day 22: Environmental Applications**
- **Starter:** Catalytic converters in cars
- **Content:** Environmental protection using kinetics
- **Discussion:** Economic vs environmental considerations
- **Activity:** Designing an environmental solution
- **Assessment:** Application questions (15 marks)
- **Homework:** Environmental impact analysis

#### **Day 23: Exam Technique Workshop**
- **Starter:** Common IEB exam mistakes
- **Content:** Reading questions, showing working, time management
- **Activity:** Marking sample answers
- **Practice:** Under exam conditions
- **Homework:** Past paper section

#### **Day 24: Revision & Integration**
- **Starter:** "What are you still unsure about?"
- **Activity:** Revision stations (5 stations, 15 min each)
  1. Calculations practice
  2. Definitions and explanations
  3. Graph interpretation
  4. Practical skills
  5. Peer teaching
- **Review:** Key concepts and formulas
- **Homework:** Comprehensive revision

#### **Day 25: Final Assessment**
- **Duration:** 1 hour
- **Marks:** 50
- **Format:** IEB-style (multiple choice, calculations, explanations, graphs)
- **Content:** All kinetics topics
- **After test:** Feedback and improvement planning

## **Differentiation Strategies:**

### **For Lower-Achieving Students:**
- Simplified worksheets with worked examples
- More visual aids and hands-on activities
- Focus on qualitative understanding first
- Peer tutoring opportunities
- Formula sheets with explanations

### **For Higher-Achieving Students:**
- Complex rate law problems
- Research on industrial kinetics
- Design of original experiments
- Participation in science competitions
- Peer teaching responsibilities

### **For English Additional Language (EAL) Students:**
- Bilingual vocabulary lists
- Sentence frames for explanations
- Visual vocabulary cards
- Partner work with language support
- Additional time for reading tasks

## **Resources Required:**

### **Equipment:**
1. Stopwatches or timers (class set)
2. Gas syringes or measuring cylinders
3. Thermometers
4. Data loggers (optional but recommended)
5. Standard laboratory glassware

### **Chemicals Needed:**
1. Sodium thiosulfate solution
2. Hydrochloric acid (various concentrations)
3. Magnesium ribbon/powder
4. Calcium carbonate (chips and powder)
5. Hydrogen peroxide (6% or 10%)
6. Manganese(IV) oxide
7. Liver or potato (catalase source)
8. Alka-Seltzer tablets

### **Safety Considerations:**
- Eye protection always worn
- Acid handling procedures followed
- Gas collection safety protocols
- Proper disposal of chemicals
- General laboratory safety rules enforced

### **Digital Resources:**
1. PhET reaction rates simulation
2. Online graph plotting tools
3. Video demonstrations of experiments
4. IEB past papers and memoranda
5. Interactive energy profile tools

## **Final Thoughts:**

Chemical kinetics provides a perfect bridge between observable phenomena and mathematical models. By starting with concrete experiences and progressing to abstract thinking, students develop both conceptual understanding and practical skills.

The key to successful IEB teaching is making connections between classroom learning and real-world applications, particularly those relevant to South African students and industries.

**Remember:** The goal is not just to calculate rates, but to understand why reactions happen at different speeds and how this knowledge shapes our world.
