# Organic Chemistry: Student Guide (Simplified)

## **Everything You Need to Know About Carbon Compounds - Made Simple!**

## **Chapter 1: What is Organic Chemistry?**

### **Simple Definition:**
**Organic chemistry = Study of carbon compounds** (except CO, CO₂, carbonates)

### **Why Carbon is Special:**
1. **Four valence electrons** → forms four covalent bonds
2. **Can bond to itself** → chains, rings, networks
3. **Forms single, double, triple bonds**
4. **Basis of life** → proteins, DNA, fats, carbohydrates all contain carbon

### **Everyday Organic Compounds:**
- **Fuels:** Petrol, diesel, natural gas
- **Food:** Sugar, starch, fats, proteins
- **Plastics:** Polyethene, PVC, polystyrene
- **Medicines:** Aspirin, paracetamol, antibiotics
- **Fabrics:** Cotton, wool, polyester, nylon

### **Memory Trick:**
```
ORGANIC = Only Really Great At Making Interesting Compounds
```

## **Chapter 2: Hydrocarbons - The Basics**

### **What are Hydrocarbons?**
- **Contain only** carbon and hydrogen
- **Three main types:** Alkanes, alkenes, alkynes

### **1. Alkanes (Single Bonds):**
- **Formula:** CₙH₂ₙ₊₂
- **All single bonds** (saturated)
- **Ending:** -ane
- **Examples:** Methane (CH₄), ethane (C₂H₆), propane (C₃H₈)

### **2. Alkenes (Double Bonds):**
- **Formula:** CₙH₂ₙ
- **Contain C=C double bond** (unsaturated)
- **Ending:** -ene
- **Examples:** Ethene (C₂H₄), propene (C₃H₆)

### **3. Alkynes (Triple Bonds):**
- **Formula:** CₙH₂ₙ₋₂
- **Contain C≡C triple bond** (unsaturated)
- **Ending:** -yne
- **Examples:** Ethyne (C₂H₂), propyne (C₃H₄)

### **Memory Trick:**
```
Alkanes: ANE = All Single Bonds
Alkenes: ENE = Double Bond Somewhere
Alkynes: YNE = Triple Bond Inside
```

## **Chapter 3: IUPAC Nomenclature**

### **Basic Naming Rules:**

#### **Step 1: Find longest carbon chain**
- Count carbons in longest continuous chain
- This gives parent name (meth-, eth-, prop-, but-, pent-, hex-, hept-, oct-, non-, dec-)

#### **Step 2: Number the chain**
- Start from end giving substituents lowest numbers
- If tie, choose direction giving next substituent lower number

#### **Step 3: Identify substituents**
- **Alkyl groups:** methyl (CH₃-), ethyl (C₂H₅-), propyl (C₃H₇-)
- **Halogens:** fluoro (F-), chloro (Cl-), bromo (Br-), iodo (I-)

#### **Step 4: Put it together**
- **Order:** Number + substituent + parent + functional group
- **Multiple same substituents:** di-, tri-, tetra-
- **Alphabetical order** (ignore prefixes di-, tri-, etc.)

### **Examples:**
1. **CH₃-CH₂-CH₃:** Propane (3 carbons, all single bonds)
2. **CH₃-CHCl-CH₃:** 2-chloropropane (chlorine on carbon 2)
3. **CH₃-CH=CH₂:** Propene (double bond, 3 carbons)
4. **CH₃-CH₂-CH₂-OH:** Propan-1-ol (OH on carbon 1)

### **Memory Trick:**
```
Name it like an address:
Number (where) + Street (substituent) + City (parent) + Type (ending)
```

## **Chapter 4: Isomerism**

### **What are Isomers?**
- **Same molecular formula**, different structures
- **Three main types:** Structural, geometric, optical

### **1. Structural Isomers:**
- **Same atoms**, different connections
- **Three subtypes:**
  - **Chain isomers:** Different carbon skeleton
  - **Position isomers:** Functional group in different position
  - **Functional group isomers:** Different functional groups

**Example (C₄H₁₀O):**
- Butan-1-ol (CH₃CH₂CH₂CH₂OH)
- Butan-2-ol (CH₃CH₂CH(OH)CH₃)
- 2-methylpropan-1-ol ((CH₃)₂CHCH₂OH)
- 2-methylpropan-2-ol ((CH₃)₃COH)
- Methoxypropane (CH₃OCH₂CH₂CH₃)

### **2. Geometric Isomers (Cis-Trans):**
- **Same connections**, different spatial arrangement
- **Requires:** Restricted rotation (double bond or ring)
- **Cis:** Same side
- **Trans:** Opposite sides

**Example:** But-2-ene
- **Cis:** CH₃ groups on same side of double bond
- **Trans:** CH₃ groups on opposite sides

### **3. Optical Isomers:**
- **Mirror images**, non-superimposable
- **Requires:** Chiral carbon (carbon with four different groups)
- **Like hands:** Left and right gloves are mirror images

### **Memory Trick:**
```
Isomers: "I So Merely" = Identical formula, So Many arrangements
Cis-Trans: "Cis = Sisters together, Trans = Across from each other"
```

## **Chapter 5: Functional Groups**

### **What are Functional Groups?**
- **Reactive part** of molecule
- **Determines properties** and reactions
- **Like "last name"** of organic compound

### **Common Functional Groups:**

#### **1. Alcohols (-OH):**
- **Ending:** -ol
- **Example:** Ethanol (CH₃CH₂OH)
- **Properties:** Polar, hydrogen bonding, soluble in water

#### **2. Aldehydes (-CHO):**
- **Ending:** -al
- **Example:** Ethanal (CH₃CHO)
- **Always at end** of chain

#### **3. Ketones (C=O):**
- **Ending:** -one
- **Example:** Propanone (CH₃COCH₃)
- **Carbonyl in middle** of chain

#### **4. Carboxylic Acids (-COOH):**
- **Ending:** -oic acid
- **Example:** Ethanoic acid (CH₃COOH)
- **Acidic properties**

#### **5. Esters (-COO-):**
- **Ending:** -oate
- **Example:** Methyl ethanoate (CH₃COOCH₃)
- **Sweet smells** (fruits, perfumes)

#### **6. Amines (-NH₂):**
- **Ending:** -amine
- **Example:** Methylamine (CH₃NH₂)
- **Basic properties**

### **Memory Trick:**
```
Alcohol: OH my!
Aldehyde: CHO (sounds like "chew" - at end)
Ketone: ONE in the middle
Acid: COOH (sounds like "cough" - acidic)
Ester: Sweet smell
Amine: NH₂ (sounds like "any two" - basic)
```

## **Chapter 6: Organic Reactions**

### **Four Main Reaction Types:**

#### **1. Substitution:**
- **Atom/group replaced** by another
- **Example:** Halogenation of alkanes
- **CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + HCl** (with UV light)

#### **2. Addition:**
- **Atoms added** to double/triple bond
- **Example:** Hydrogenation of alkenes
- **CH₂=CH₂ + H₂ → CH₃-CH₃** (with Ni catalyst)

#### **3. Elimination:**
- **Atoms removed** to form double bond
- **Example:** Dehydration of alcohols
- **CH₃CH₂OH → CH₂=CH₂ + H₂O** (with conc. H₂SO₄)

#### **4. Oxidation/Reduction:**
- **Change in oxygen/hydrogen** content
- **Example:** Alcohol oxidation
- **Primary alcohol → aldehyde → carboxylic acid**

### **Common Reaction Examples:**

#### **Combustion:**
- **Hydrocarbon + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O**
- **Example:** CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O

#### **Esterification:**
- **Acid + alcohol → ester + water**
- **Example:** CH₃COOH + CH₃OH → CH₃COOCH₃ + H₂O

#### **Hydrolysis:**
- **Ester + water → acid + alcohol** (reverse of esterification)
- **Example:** CH₃COOCH₃ + H₂O → CH₃COOH + CH₃OH

### **Memory Trick:**
```
Substitution: Swap partners
Addition: Add to double date
Elimination: Break up to be single
Oxidation: Gain oxygen or lose hydrogen
```

## **Chapter 7: Polymers**

### **What are Polymers?**
- **Large molecules** from repeating units (monomers)
- **"Poly" = many, "mer" = parts**
- **Two main types:** Addition and condensation

### **1. Addition Polymers:**
- **Chain growth** polymerization
- **Monomer has C=C double bond**
- **Example:** Polyethene from ethene
- **nCH₂=CH₂ → (-CH₂-CH₂-)ₙ**

### **Common Addition Polymers:**
- **Polyethene (PE):** Plastic bags, bottles
- **Polypropene (PP):** Food containers, carpets
- **Polyvinyl chloride (PVC):** Pipes, credit cards
- **Polystyrene (PS):** Packaging, disposable cups

### **2. Condensation Polymers:**
- **Step growth** polymerization
- **Two different monomers** with two functional groups each
- **Lose small molecule** (usually water)
- **Example:** Polyester, nylon

### **Common Condensation Polymers:**
- **Polyester:** Clothing, bottles
- **Nylon:** Clothing, ropes, carpets
- **Proteins:** Natural polymers from amino acids
- **DNA/RNA:** Natural polymers from nucleotides

### **Memory Trick:**
```
Addition: Add monomers together (like adding links to chain)
Condensation: Condense out water (like squeezing water from sponge)
```

## **Chapter 8: Common Exam Questions**

### **Question Type 1: Nomenclature**
**Example:** Name CH₃CH₂CH(OH)CH₃
**Solution:** Butan-2-ol (4 carbons = but-, OH on carbon 2 = -2-ol)

### **Question Type 2: Isomer Drawing**
**Example:** Draw all structural isomers of C₄H₁₀O that are alcohols.
**Solution:** Butan-1-ol, butan-2-ol, 2-methylpropan-1-ol, 2-methylpropan-2-ol

### **Question Type 3: Reaction Prediction**
**Example:** Predict product of CH₃CH=CH₂ + HBr
**Solution:** CH₃CHBrCH₃ (Markovnikov addition: H adds to C with more H's)

### **Question Type 4: Functional Group Identification**
**Example:** Identify functional groups in aspirin:
```
   O
  ║
O-C-CH₃
  |
COOH
```
**Solution:** Carboxylic acid (-COOH) and ester (-COO-)

### **Question Type 5: Polymerization**
**Example:** Show polymerization of ethene to polyethene.
**Solution:** nCH₂=CH₂ → (-CH₂-CH₂-)ₙ

## **Chapter 9: Study Tips & Tricks**

### **Memory Aids:**
- **First 10 alkanes:** "Monkeys Eat Peanut Butter, People Have Only Nine Donuts" (Meth-, Eth-, Prop-, But-, Pent-, Hex-, Hept-, Oct-, Non-, Dec-)
- **Functional groups:** Make flashcards with names, formulas, examples
- **Reaction types:** "SANE" = Substitution, Addition, Nucleophilic, Elimination

### **Formula Sheet (Memorize These):**
1. **Alkanes:** CₙH₂ₙ₊₂
2. **Alkenes:** CₙH₂ₙ
3. **Alkynes:** CₙH₂ₙ₋₂
4. **Alcohols:** R-OH
5. **Aldehydes:** R-CHO
6. **Ketones:** R-CO-R'
7. **Carboxylic acids:** R-COOH
8. **Esters:** R-COO-R'

### **Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Forgetting to number the chain correctly
- Not counting longest continuous chain
- Confusing aldehydes and ketones (aldehyde at end, ketone in middle)
- Incorrectly naming branched chains
- Forgetting geometric isomerism when double bond present

### **Problem-Solving Steps:**
1. **Count carbons** in longest chain
2. **Identify functional group** (determines ending)
3. **Number chain** from end giving lowest numbers
4. **Name substituents** in alphabetical order
5. **Check for isomerism** if asked
6. **Write final name** with correct punctuation
