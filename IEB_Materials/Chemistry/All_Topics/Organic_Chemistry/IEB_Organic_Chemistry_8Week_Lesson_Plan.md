# IEB Physical Sciences: 8-Week Organic Chemistry Lesson Plan (Grade 12)

## **Complete Teaching Schedule with Daily Activities, Assessments, and Resources**

---

## **Week 1: Introduction & Hydrocarbons**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand carbon's unique bonding properties
- Name and draw simple hydrocarbons
- Distinguish between alkanes, alkenes, and alkynes
- Understand homologous series and trends

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 1: Introduction to Organic Chemistry**
- **Starter:** "What contains carbon?" brainstorming
- **Content:** Organic vs inorganic, carbon's special properties
- **Importance:** Life, materials, energy, medicine
- **Activity:** Identifying organic compounds in everyday life
- **Demo:** Combustion of organic vs inorganic compounds
- **Homework:** Find 10 organic compounds at home

#### **Day 2: Alkanes - Saturated Hydrocarbons**
- **Starter:** What are fossil fuels made of?
- **Content:** Alkanes: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂, single bonds, saturated
- **First 10 alkanes:** Methane to decane
- **Activity:** Building alkane models (physical or paper)
- **Practice:** Drawing and naming simple alkanes
- **Homework:** Alkane naming worksheet

#### **Day 3: Alkenes & Alkynes**
- **Starter:** How are plastics different from fuels?
- **Content:** Alkenes (CₙH₂ₙ, C=C), alkynes (CₙH₂ₙ₋₂, C≡C)
- **Unsaturated vs saturated**
- **Activity:** Comparing properties of alkanes, alkenes, alkynes
- **Practice:** Naming and drawing unsaturated hydrocarbons
- **Assessment:** Hydrocarbon basics quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Unsaturated hydrocarbon worksheet

#### **Day 4: Homologous Series & Trends**
- **Starter:** Pattern in boiling points of alkanes?
- **Content:** Homologous series concept
- **Trends:** Boiling points, solubility, reactivity
- **Activity:** Graphing alkane boiling points vs carbon number
- **Analysis:** Explaining trends using intermolecular forces
- **Homework:** Homologous series analysis

#### **Day 5: Petroleum & Combustion**
- **Starter:** Where do hydrocarbons come from?
- **Content:** Petroleum formation, fractional distillation
- **Combustion:** Complete vs incomplete, environmental impact
- **Activity:** Fractional distillation simulation/diagram
- **Discussion:** Fossil fuels and climate change
- **Homework:** Petroleum industry research

---

## **Week 2: IUPAC Nomenclature**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Apply IUPAC naming rules systematically
- Name branched-chain hydrocarbons
- Name compounds with functional groups
- Draw structures from names

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 6: Basic Nomenclature Rules**
- **Starter:** Why do we need systematic naming?
- **Content:** IUPAC rules overview
- **Steps:** Find longest chain, number, name substituents, assemble
- **Activity:** Step-by-step naming examples
- **Practice:** Simple branched alkane naming
- **Homework:** Basic nomenclature worksheet

#### **Day 7: Alkyl Groups & Halogen Substituents**
- **Starter:** What are methyl, ethyl, propyl groups?
- **Content:** Common alkyl groups, halogen prefixes (fluoro, chloro, etc.)
- **Multiple substituents:** di-, tri-, tetra-
- **Activity:** Naming compounds with multiple substituents
- **Practice:** Various substitution patterns
- **Homework:** Substituted hydrocarbon naming

#### **Day 8: Functional Group Priority**
- **Starter:** What if compound has multiple functional groups?
- **Content:** Functional group priority order
- **Determining parent chain:** Longest chain containing highest priority group
- **Activity:** Priority determination exercises
- **Assessment:** Nomenclature quiz (15 marks)
- **Homework:** Priority rule application

#### **Day 9: Complex Nomenclature**
- **Starter:** Challenging naming examples
- **Content:** Cyclic compounds, multiple functional groups
- **Activity:** Workshop on difficult naming problems
- **Practice:** Peer teaching and checking
- **Homework:** Complex nomenclature problems

#### **Day 10: Drawing from Names**
- **Starter:** Reverse process: name → structure
- **Content:** Systematic approach to drawing
- **Activity:** Drawing structures from IUPAC names
- **Practice:** Various complexity levels
- **Assessment:** Weekly test (20 marks)
- **Reflection:** "Which naming rule was hardest?"

## **Week 3: Isomerism**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Define and identify different types of isomers
- Draw structural isomers for given formulas
- Understand geometric (cis-trans) isomerism
- Introduce optical isomerism concepts

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 11: Structural Isomerism Introduction**
- **Starter:** Same formula, different properties?
- **Content:** Structural isomer definition and types
- **Three types:** Chain, position, functional group
- **Activity:** Finding isomers for simple formulas
- **Practice:** Isomer drawing exercises
- **Homework:** Structural isomer worksheet

#### **Day 12: Chain & Position Isomers**
- **Starter:** Different skeletons, same formula
- **Content:** Chain isomer examples, position isomer examples
- **Activity:** Drawing all isomers for C₄H₁₀, C₄H₈, C₄H₆
- **Practice:** Systematic isomer finding method
- **Homework:** Chain and position isomer problems

#### **Day 13: Functional Group Isomers**
- **Starter:** Same atoms, different functional groups
- **Content:** Examples: alcohols/ethers, aldehydes/ketones
- **Activity:** Finding functional group isomers
- **Practice:** C₃H₆O, C₄H₈O₂ examples
- **Assessment:** Isomerism quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Functional group isomer worksheet

#### **Day 14: Geometric Isomerism (Cis-Trans)**
- **Starter:** Same connections, different shapes
- **Content:** Geometric isomer conditions, cis vs trans
- **Requirements:** Restricted rotation (double bond or ring)
- **Activity:** Building cis-trans models
- **Practice:** Identifying and drawing geometric isomers
- **Homework:** Geometric isomer problems

#### **Day 15: Optical Isomerism Introduction**
- **Starter:** Mirror images that don't match
- **Content:** Chiral carbon, enantiomers, optical activity
- **Biological importance:** Drug effectiveness
- **Activity:** Hand analogy, model building
- **Discussion:** Thalidomide case study
- **Homework:** Optical isomer basics

## **Week 4: Functional Groups & Properties**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Identify common functional groups
- Understand properties conferred by functional groups
- Predict physical properties based on structure
- Relate structure to solubility and boiling points

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 16: Alcohols**
- **Starter:** What makes alcohols different from alkanes?
- **Content:** -OH group, hydrogen bonding, classification (1°, 2°, 3°)
- **Properties:** Solubility, boiling points, reactivity
- **Activity:** Comparing alcohol vs alkane properties
- **Practice:** Naming and drawing alcohols
- **Homework:** Alcohol properties worksheet

#### **Day 17: Aldehydes & Ketones**
- **Starter:** Sweet smells and flavors
- **Content:** Carbonyl group, aldehyde vs ketone differences
- **Properties:** Polarity, boiling points, reactivity
- **Activity:** Identifying aldehydes and ketones in products
- **Practice:** Naming and drawing carbonyl compounds
- **Homework:** Aldehyde/ketone worksheet

#### **Day 18: Carboxylic Acids**
- **Starter:** Why is vinegar acidic?
- **Content:** -COOH group, acidity, hydrogen bonding
- **Properties:** Solubility, boiling points, acidity strength
- **Activity:** Testing acidity of organic acids
- **Assessment:** Functional groups quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Carboxylic acid problems

#### **Day 19: Esters & Amines**
- **Starter:** Fruity smells and basic compounds
- **Content:** Esters (fragrances), amines (basic, fishy smell)
- **Properties:** Ester smells, amine basicity
- **Activity:** Ester synthesis demo (smell production)
- **Practice:** Naming esters and amines
- **Homework:** Ester and amine worksheet

#### **Day 20: Physical Properties Trends**
- **Starter:** Predicting boiling points from structure
- **Content:** Intermolecular forces in organic compounds
- **Trends:** Chain length, branching, functional groups
- **Activity:** Data analysis of organic compound properties
- **Discussion:** Structure-property relationships
- **Homework:** Property prediction exercises

---

## **Week 5: Organic Reactions**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand four main organic reaction types
- Predict products of common organic reactions
- Write balanced equations for organic reactions
- Apply reaction knowledge to synthesis problems

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 21: Substitution Reactions**
- **Starter:** Swapping atoms in molecules
- **Content:** Halogenation of alkanes, nucleophilic substitution
- **Conditions:** UV light for alkanes, different mechanisms
- **Activity:** Writing substitution reaction equations
- **Practice:** Predicting substitution products
- **Homework:** Substitution reaction worksheet

#### **Day 22: Addition Reactions**
- **Starter:** Adding to double bonds
- **Content:** Hydrogenation, halogenation, hydration of alkenes
- **Markovnikov's rule:** H adds to C with more H's
- **Activity:** Addition reaction examples and mechanisms
- **Practice:** Predicting addition products
- **Assessment:** Organic reactions quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Addition reaction problems

#### **Day 23: Elimination Reactions**
- **Starter:** Removing atoms to make double bonds
- **Content:** Dehydration of alcohols, dehydrohalogenation
- **Zaitsev's rule:** More substituted alkene favored
- **Activity:** Elimination reaction examples
- **Practice:** Predicting elimination products
- **Homework:** Elimination reaction worksheet

#### **Day 24: Oxidation & Reduction**
- **Starter:** Changing oxygen/hydrogen content
- **Content:** Alcohol oxidation (1°→aldehyde→acid, 2°→ketone)
- **Reducing agents:** LiAlH₄, NaBH₄
- **Activity:** Oxidation state changes in organic compounds
- **Practice:** Oxidation/reduction predictions
- **Homework:** Oxidation/reduction problems

#### **Day 25: Practical Session 1**
- **Title:** "Ester Synthesis"
- **Experiment:** Prepare an ester from alcohol and carboxylic acid
- **Observation:** Smell production, purification
- **Skills:** Synthesis, purification, observation
- **Assessment:** Lab report (20 marks)
- **Extension:** Different ester preparations

---

## **Week 6: Reaction Mechanisms & Synthesis**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand basic reaction mechanisms
- Apply reaction knowledge to multi-step synthesis
- Design simple synthetic routes
- Analyze reaction pathways

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 26: Reaction Mechanisms Introduction**
- **Starter:** How do reactions actually happen?
- **Content:** Curly arrows, intermediates, transition states
- **Simple mechanisms:** Free radical substitution, electrophilic addition
- **Activity:** Drawing mechanism steps
- **Practice:** Simple mechanism exercises
- **Homework:** Mechanism basics worksheet

#### **Day 27: Multi-step Synthesis**
- **Starter:** Building complex molecules step by step
- **Content:** Synthetic strategy, retrosynthetic analysis
- **Activity:** Planning synthesis of simple target molecules
- **Practice:** Two- and three-step syntheses
- **Assessment:** Synthesis quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Synthesis planning problems

#### **Day 28: Functional Group Interconversions**
- **Starter:** Changing one functional group to another
- **Content:** Common interconversion pathways
- **Examples:** Alkene → alcohol → aldehyde → acid
- **Activity:** Mapping interconversion pathways
- **Practice:** Designing interconversion sequences
- **Homework:** Interconversion worksheet

#### **Day 29: Green Chemistry & Sustainability**
- **Starter:** Environmental impact of organic chemistry
- **Content:** Green chemistry principles, atom economy
- **Sustainable synthesis:** Biocatalysis, renewable feedstocks
- **Activity:** Analyzing synthesis routes for greenness
- **Discussion:** Future of sustainable organic chemistry
- **Homework:** Green chemistry case study

#### **Day 30: Practical Session 2**
- **Title:** "Alcohol Oxidation"
- **Experiment:** Oxidize primary alcohol to aldehyde/carboxylic acid
- **Testing:** Use oxidizing agents, test products
- **Skills:** Oxidation reactions, product testing
- **Assessment:** Lab report (20 marks)
- **Extension:** Different alcohol oxidations

## **Week 7: Polymers & Applications**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand polymerization processes
- Distinguish between addition and condensation polymers
- Identify common polymers and their uses
- Apply polymer knowledge to real-world applications

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 31: Addition Polymers**
- **Starter:** What are plastics made of?
- **Content:** Chain-growth polymerization, monomer with C=C
- **Examples:** Polyethene, polypropene, PVC, polystyrene
- **Activity:** Building polymer models from monomers
- **Practice:** Writing polymerization equations
- **Homework:** Addition polymer worksheet

#### **Day 32: Condensation Polymers**
- **Starter:** How are nylon and polyester made?
- **Content:** Step-growth polymerization, loss of small molecule
- **Examples:** Polyester, nylon, proteins, DNA
- **Activity:** Condensation polymer formation demonstrations
- **Practice:** Writing condensation polymerization equations
- **Assessment:** Polymers quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Condensation polymer problems

#### **Day 33: Natural Polymers**
- **Starter:** Polymers in nature
- **Content:** Proteins, carbohydrates, nucleic acids
- **Structure-function relationships**
- **Activity:** Comparing natural and synthetic polymers
- **Discussion:** Biomimicry in materials science
- **Homework:** Natural polymer research

#### **Day 34: Polymer Properties & Uses**
- **Starter:** Why are polymers so useful?
- **Content:** Structure-property relationships in polymers
- **Applications:** Packaging, textiles, construction, medicine
- **Activity:** Polymer property testing (strength, flexibility, etc.)
- **Practice:** Matching polymers to applications
- **Homework:** Polymer applications worksheet

#### **Day 35: Environmental Impact of Polymers**
- **Starter:** Plastic pollution problem
- **Content:** Biodegradable vs non-biodegradable polymers
- **Solutions:** Recycling, bioplastics, circular economy
- **Activity:** Life cycle analysis of plastic products
- **Discussion:** Sustainable polymer use
- **Homework:** Environmental impact assessment

## **Week 8: Integration & Exam Preparation**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Solve complex integrated organic chemistry problems
- Apply multiple concepts to synthesis and analysis
- Prepare for IEB examinations
- Develop exam technique for organic chemistry questions

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 36: Complex Problem Solving**
- **Starter:** Multi-concept organic chemistry problem
- **Content:** Integrating nomenclature, isomerism, reactions, synthesis
- **Strategy:** Systematic problem-solving approach
- **Activity:** Worked complex examples
- **Practice:** Independent problem solving
- **Homework:** Complex organic problems

#### **Day 37: Exam Technique Workshop**
- **Starter:** Common IEB organic chemistry question types
- **Content:** Reading questions, showing working, time management
- **Activity:** Marking sample answers
- **Practice:** Under exam conditions
- **Homework:** Past paper section

#### **Day 38: Revision Stations**
- **Setup:** 5 stations (15 min each)
  1. Nomenclature practice
  2. Isomer drawing and identification
  3. Reaction prediction and mechanisms
  4. Synthesis planning
  5. Polymer concepts and applications
- **Activity:** Rotate through stations
- **Review:** Key concepts and formulas
- **Homework:** Comprehensive revision

#### **Day 39: Past Paper Practice**
- **Paper:** IEB 2023 Paper 2 (organic chemistry section)
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Marks:** 30
- **Activity:** Under exam conditions
- **After:** Self-mark using memorandum
- **Homework:** Error correction and improvement

#### **Day 40: Final Assessment**
- **Duration:** 1 hour
- **Marks:** 50
- **Format:** IEB-style (multiple choice, nomenclature, isomer drawing, reaction prediction, synthesis, applications)
- **Content:** All organic chemistry topics
- **After test:** Feedback and improvement planning

## **Differentiation Strategies:**

### **For Lower-Achieving Students:**
- Simplified worksheets with worked examples
- More visual aids and molecular models
- Focus on pattern recognition first
- Peer tutoring opportunities
- Cheat sheets for nomenclature rules

### **For Higher-Achieving Students:**
- Complex synthesis problems
- Research on pharmaceutical synthesis
- Design of organic experiments
- Participation in science competitions
- Peer teaching responsibilities

### **For English Additional Language (EAL) Students:**
- Bilingual vocabulary lists
- Sentence frames for explanations
- Visual vocabulary cards
- Partner work with language support
- Additional time for reading tasks
