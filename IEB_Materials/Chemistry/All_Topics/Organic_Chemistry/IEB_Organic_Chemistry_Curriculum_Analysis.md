# IEB Physical Sciences: Organic Chemistry Curriculum Analysis

## **Based on IEB Physical Sciences Curriculum and Assessment Manual 2024**

---

## **Part 1: IEB Curriculum Requirements for Organic Chemistry**

### **Grade 10-12 Progression:**

#### **Grade 10 (Introduction):**
- **Topic:** Organic compounds
- **Content:**
  - What are organic compounds?
  - Hydrocarbons (alkanes, alkenes, alkynes)
  - Simple functional groups (alcohols, carboxylic acids)
  - Everyday organic compounds
  - Combustion of hydrocarbons

#### **Grade 11 (Development):**
- **Topic:** Organic molecules
- **Content:**
  - IUPAC nomenclature (up to C10)
  - Isomerism (structural only)
  - Homologous series
  - Physical properties trends
  - Simple reactions (addition, substitution)

#### **Grade 12 (Advanced):**
- **Topic:** Organic chemistry
- **Content:**
  - Advanced nomenclature (including branched chains)
  - Isomerism (structural, geometric, optical intro)
  - Functional groups and their reactions
  - Reaction mechanisms (substitution, addition, elimination)
  - Polymers and their importance
  - Applications in medicine, industry, environment

## **Part 2: IEB Assessment Standards**

### **Cognitive Levels:**
1. **Knowledge (20%):** Name compounds, recall formulas, identify functional groups
2. **Comprehension (25%):** Explain trends, interpret structures, describe reactions
3. **Application (30%):** Draw structures, predict products, name compounds
4. **Analysis (15%):** Analyze reaction pathways, compare isomers
5. **Evaluation (10%):** Evaluate environmental impact, assess synthetic routes

### **Assessment Types:**
- **Tests (40%):** Nomenclature, reaction prediction, structure drawing
- **Practical Investigations (20%):** Organic synthesis, property testing
- **Projects (15%):** Polymer research, pharmaceutical applications
- **Examinations (25%):** Paper 2 questions

### **IEB Exam Structure (Paper 2 - Chemistry):**
- **Total marks:** 150
- **Organic chemistry typically:** 25-35 marks
- **Question types:** Multiple choice, nomenclature, reaction equations, mechanism steps, structure drawing, application questions

## **Part 5: Key Concepts Simplified**

### **1. Carbon's Uniqueness:**
- **Four valence electrons** → forms four covalent bonds
- **Can bond to itself** → chains, rings, networks
- **Variety of bonds** → single, double, triple
- **Basis of life** → all living things contain carbon

### **2. Homologous Series:**
- **Family of compounds** with same functional group
- **General formula** (e.g., alkanes: CₙH₂ₙ₊₂)
- **Gradual change** in physical properties
- **Similar chemical properties**

### **3. Functional Groups:**
- **Reactive part** of molecule
- **Determines properties** and reactions
- **Common groups:** -OH (alcohol), -COOH (acid), -CHO (aldehyde), C=O (ketone), -NH₂ (amine)

### **4. Isomerism:**

#### **Structural Isomers:**
- **Same formula**, different structure
- **Types:** Chain, position, functional group

#### **Geometric Isomers (Cis-Trans):**
- **Same connections**, different spatial arrangement
- **Requires** double bond or ring
- **Cis** = same side, **Trans** = opposite sides

#### **Optical Isomers:**
- **Mirror images**, non-superimposable
- **Chiral carbon** = carbon with four different groups
- **Important** in pharmaceuticals

### **5. Reaction Types:**

#### **Substitution:**
- **Atom/group replaced** by another
- **Example:** Halogenation of alkanes

#### **Addition:**
- **Atoms added** to double/triple bond
- **Example:** Hydrogenation of alkenes

#### **Elimination:**
- **Atoms removed** to form double bond
- **Example:** Dehydration of alcohols

#### **Oxidation/Reduction:**
- **Change in oxygen/hydrogen** content
- **Example:** Alcohol → aldehyde → carboxylic acid

### **6. Polymers:**
- **Large molecules** from repeating units (monomers)
- **Addition polymers:** Chain growth (polyethene, PVC)
- **Condensation polymers:** Step growth with loss of small molecule (polyester, nylon)

## **Part 6: Common Student Misconceptions**

### **Misconception 1:** "Organic = natural, inorganic = artificial"
- **Correction:** Organic means carbon-based (can be natural or synthetic)

### **Misconception 2:** "All organic compounds come from living things"
- **Correction:** Can be synthesized in lab (urea first synthetic organic compound)

### **Misconception 3:** "Structural formulas show 3D shape"
- **Correction:** Structural formulas show connections, need models for 3D

### **Misconception 4:** "Isomers have different formulas"
- **Correction:** Isomers have SAME molecular formula

### **Misconception 5:** "Polymers are all plastics"
- **Correction:** Polymers include proteins, DNA, rubber, etc.

### **1. Start with Everyday Examples:**
- Household organic compounds (fuels, plastics, medicines)
- Food chemistry (fats, sugars, proteins)
- Personal care products (soaps, perfumes)

### **2. Use Molecular Models:**
- Ball-and-stick models for 3D visualization

- Paper models for isomer drawing
- Emphasize spatial understanding

### **3. Pattern Recognition Approach:**
- Teach functional group patterns
- Reaction type patterns
- Nomenclature patterns
- Build from simple to complex

### **4. Practical Integration:**
- Minimum 3 organic experiments
- Include synthesis, testing, analysis
- Emphasize safety with organic chemicals
- Develop scientific reporting

### **5. Contextual Learning:**
- South African context (SASOL, pharmaceutical industry)
- Biological context (biochemistry, medicines)
- Environmental context (plastics, pollution)
- Technological context (materials, nanotechnology)

## **Part 9: IEB Exam Question Types**

### **Type A: Multiple Choice (Knowledge)**
**Example:** Which is an alcohol functional group?
A) -COOH  
B) -OH  
C) -CHO  
D) -NH₂  
**Answer:** B

### **Type B: Nomenclature (Application)**
**Example:** Name CH₃CH₂CH(OH)CH₃
**Solution:** Butan-2-ol

### **Type C: Reaction Prediction (Application)**
**Example:** Predict product of CH₃CH=CH₂ + HBr
**Solution:** CH₃CHBrCH₃ (Markovnikov addition)

### **Type D: Isomer Drawing (Analysis)**
**Example:** Draw all structural isomers of C₄H₁₀O that are alcohols.
**Solution:** Butan-1-ol, butan-2-ol, 2-methylpropan-1-ol, 2-methylpropan-2-ol

### **Type E: Applications (Evaluation)**
**Example:** Discuss environmental impact of plastic polymers.
**Answer:** Non-biodegradable, pollution, recycling challenges, alternatives.

## **Part 12: Sample Lesson Plan (IEB Grade 12)**

### **Lesson: Geometric Isomerism**
**Duration:** 1 hour

#### **Learning Objectives:**
- Define geometric (cis-trans) isomerism
- Identify conditions for geometric isomerism
- Draw and name geometric isomers

#### **Resources:**
- Molecular model kits
- Worksheets with isomer problems
- Whiteboard for examples

#### **Lesson Structure:**

**Starter (5 min):**
- Quick review: What are isomers?
- Show cis-trans butter vs margarine example

**Direct Instruction (15 min):**
- Define geometric isomerism
- Conditions: restricted rotation (double bond or ring)
- Cis vs trans definitions
- Show examples with models

**Guided Practice (20 min):**
- Work through examples on board
- Step-by-step: Identify if geometric isomerism possible, draw isomers
- Common mistakes highlighted

**Independent Practice (15 min):**
- Worksheet with 4 compounds to analyze
- Determine if geometric isomers possible, draw if yes
- Use model kits for 3D understanding

**Plenary (5 min):**
- Key takeaway: Need restricted rotation for geometric isomers
- Exit ticket: "What two conditions allow geometric isomerism?"

#### **Homework:**
- Textbook problems on geometric isomerism
- Find examples of cis-trans isomers in everyday life

#### **Assessment:**
- Formative: Worksheet completion
- Summative: Isomer question in next test

## **Conclusion**

Organic chemistry provides the foundation for understanding the molecular basis of life and modern materials. By connecting structural concepts with practical applications in medicine, industry, and environment, students develop both spatial reasoning and practical problem-solving skills.

The key to successful IEB teaching is balancing theoretical understanding with practical applications, particularly those relevant to South African context and student futures in science and technology.

**Remember:** The goal is not just to name compounds, but to understand how organic molecules structure our world and how we can design new molecules to improve it.
