## **Week 1: Acid-Base Fundamentals & pH Calculations**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Define acids and bases using different theories
- Understand the pH scale and its significance
- Calculate pH for strong acid/base solutions
- Distinguish between strong and weak acids/bases

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 1: Introduction to Acids and Bases**
- **Starter:** "Acid or base?" household substance testing
- **Content:** Properties of acids and bases
- **Theories:** Arrhenius introduction
- **Demo:** Litmus paper testing, reaction with metals
- **Activity:** Classifying substances as acid/base
- **Homework:** Find 5 acids and 5 bases at home

#### **Day 2: Br√∏nsted-Lowry Theory**
- **Starter:** Limitations of Arrhenius theory
- **Content:** Proton donor/acceptor concept
- **Conjugate pairs:** Acid/base pairs relationship
- **Activity:** Identifying conjugate pairs in reactions
- **Practice:** Writing conjugate acid-base pairs
- **Homework:** Conjugate pairs worksheet

#### **Day 3: pH Scale Introduction**
- **Starter:** What does pH mean? (Brainstorm)
- **Content:** pH definition, scale 0-14
- **Relationship:** pH = -log[H‚Å∫], pH + pOH = 14
- **Activity:** pH of common substances matching
- **Homework:** pH scale worksheet

#### **Day 4: Strong Acid/Base pH Calculations**
- **Starter:** Strong vs weak distinction
- **Content:** Complete dissociation of strong acids/bases
- **Calculations:** pH = -log[H‚Å∫] for acids, pOH = -log[OH‚Åª] then pH = 14-pOH for bases
- **Activity:** Calculation practice with worked examples
- **Differentiation:** Tiered worksheets
- **Homework:** Strong acid/base calculations

#### **Day 5: Weak Acid/Base Introduction**
- **Starter:** Why is vinegar weak acid?
- **Content:** Partial dissociation, equilibrium concept
- **Ka and Kb:** Introduction to acid/base dissociation constants
- **Activity:** Comparing strong vs weak properties
- **Assessment:** Acid-base fundamentals quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Weak acid/base properties

## **Week 3: Titrations & Indicators**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand titration principles and setup
- Perform titration calculations
- Select appropriate indicators for titrations
- Draw and interpret titration curves

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 11: Titration Introduction**
- **Starter:** How do we find unknown concentration?
- **Content:** Titration principles and equipment
- **Setup:** Burette, pipette, flask, indicator
- **Demo:** Simple acid-base titration
- **Activity:** Labeling titration apparatus
- **Homework:** Titration equipment worksheet

#### **Day 12: Titration Calculations**
- **Starter:** Moles acid = moles base at equivalence point
- **Content:** M‚ÇÅV‚ÇÅ = M‚ÇÇV‚ÇÇ formula (for 1:1 ratio)
- **General formula:** n‚ÇÅM‚ÇÅV‚ÇÅ = n‚ÇÇM‚ÇÇV‚ÇÇ (accounting for stoichiometry)
- **Activity:** Calculation practice with various ratios
- **Practice:** Titration calculation problems
- **Homework:** Titration calculations worksheet

#### **Day 13: Indicators**
- **Starter:** Why do indicators change color?
- **Content:** Indicator theory and pH ranges
- **Common indicators:** Phenolphthalein, methyl orange, universal
- **Selection:** Choosing right indicator for titration type
- **Activity:** Indicator pH range matching
- **Assessment:** Titration concepts quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Indicator selection exercises

#### **Day 14: Titration Curves**
- **Starter:** What does a titration curve show?
- **Content:** pH vs volume added, equivalence point, half-equivalence point
- **Types:** Strong acid-strong base, weak acid-strong base, etc.
- **Activity:** Drawing and interpreting titration curves
- **Practice:** Curve analysis problems
- **Homework:** Titration curve worksheet

#### **Day 15: Practical Session 1**
- **Title:** "Acid-Base Titration"
- **Experiment:** Standardization of NaOH with KHP
- **Determination:** Unknown acid concentration
- **Skills:** Burette use, endpoint detection, calculation
- **Assessment:** Lab report (20 marks)
- **Extension:** Error analysis and improvement

## **Week 4: Buffer Solutions**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Define buffer solutions and their properties
- Explain buffer action mechanism
- Calculate buffer pH using Henderson-Hasselbalch equation
- Prepare and test buffer solutions

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 16: Buffer Introduction**
- **Starter:** How does blood maintain constant pH?
- **Content:** Buffer definition and components
- **Types:** Acid buffer (weak acid + salt), base buffer (weak base + salt)
- **Activity:** Identifying buffer systems
- **Homework:** Buffer basics worksheet

#### **Day 17: Buffer Action Mechanism**
- **Starter:** How do buffers resist pH change?
- **Content:** Acid attack: H‚Å∫ + A‚Åª ‚Üí HA, Base attack: OH‚Åª + HA ‚Üí A‚Åª + H‚ÇÇO
- **Visual:** Particle model of buffer action
- **Activity:** Explaining buffer action in different scenarios
- **Practice:** Buffer action explanations
- **Homework:** Buffer mechanism worksheet

#### **Day 18: Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation**
- **Starter:** Mathematical description of buffers
- **Content:** pH = pKa + log([salt]/[acid])
- **Derivation:** From Ka expression
- **Activity:** Equation application examples
- **Practice:** Buffer pH calculations
- **Assessment:** Buffer calculations quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Henderson-Hasselbalch problems

#### **Day 19: Buffer Capacity and Preparation**
- **Starter:** How much acid/base can a buffer handle?
- **Content:** Buffer capacity concept, optimal pH range (pKa ¬± 1)
- **Preparation:** How to make buffer solutions
- **Activity:** Calculating amounts for buffer preparation
- **Practice:** Buffer preparation problems
- **Homework:** Buffer preparation worksheet

#### **Day 20: Biological Buffers**
- **Starter:** Buffers in living systems
- **Content:** Blood buffer system (H‚ÇÇCO‚ÇÉ/HCO‚ÇÉ‚Åª)
- **Application:** Acidosis and alkalosis, respiration effects
- **Activity:** Analyzing blood buffer system
- **Discussion:** Medical importance of pH balance
- **Homework:** Biological buffer research

## **Week 5: Salt Hydrolysis & Applications**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Predict pH of salt solutions
- Explain salt hydrolysis mechanisms
- Apply acid-base concepts to industrial processes
- Understand environmental acid-base issues

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 21: Salt Hydrolysis Introduction**
- **Starter:** Are all salt solutions neutral?
- **Content:** Salt hydrolysis definition and types
- **Four categories:** Strong acid-strong base, strong acid-weak base, weak acid-strong base, weak acid-weak base
- **Activity:** Predicting salt pH from parent acids/bases
- **Homework:** Salt hydrolysis predictions

#### **Day 22: Hydrolysis Calculations**
- **Starter:** Quantitative salt hydrolysis
- **Content:** Using Ka/Kb of conjugate acids/bases
- **Examples:** NH‚ÇÑCl (acidic), CH‚ÇÉCOONa (basic)
- **Activity:** Calculation practice
- **Practice:** Various salt hydrolysis problems
- **Assessment:** Salt hydrolysis quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Hydrolysis calculations

#### **Day 23: Industrial Applications**
- **Starter:** Acids and bases in industry
- **Content:** Fertilizer production, mining, food industry
- **South African focus:** Acid mine drainage, SASOL processes
- **Activity:** Case study analysis
- **Discussion:** Economic and environmental considerations
- **Homework:** Industrial application research

#### **Day 24: Environmental Applications**
- **Starter:** Acid rain and ocean acidification
- **Content:** Environmental pH issues
- **Mechanisms:** CO‚ÇÇ absorption, sulfur/nitrogen oxides
- **Impact:** Ecosystems, buildings, human health
- **Activity:** Environmental problem analysis
- **Homework:** Environmental impact assessment

#### **Day 25: Practical Session 2**
- **Title:** "Buffer Preparation and Testing"
- **Experiment:** Prepare acetate buffer, test capacity
- **Testing:** Add acid/base, measure pH changes
- **Skills:** Buffer preparation, pH measurement, capacity testing
- **Assessment:** Lab report (20 marks)
- **Extension:** Compare different buffer systems

## **Differentiation Strategies:**

### **For Lower-Achieving Students:**
- Simplified worksheets with worked examples
- More visual aids and hands-on pH testing
- Focus on qualitative understanding first
- Peer tutoring opportunities
- Formula sheets with explanations

### **For Higher-Achieving Students:**
- Complex titration and buffer problems
- Research on advanced acid-base theories
- Design of improved titration methods
- Participation in science competitions
- Peer teaching responsibilities

### **For English Additional Language (EAL) Students:**
- Bilingual vocabulary lists
- Sentence frames for explanations
- Visual vocabulary cards
- Partner work with language support
- Additional time for reading tasks

## **Final Thoughts:**

Acids and bases provide a crucial link between theoretical chemistry and practical applications in biology, industry, and environment. By connecting mathematical calculations with sensory experiences and real-world applications, students develop both conceptual understanding and practical skills.

The key to successful IEB teaching is balancing theoretical understanding with practical applications, particularly those relevant to South African context and student experiences.

**Remember:** The goal is not just to calculate pH values, but to understand acid-base behavior in natural and industrial systems, and how this knowledge impacts our world, health, and environment.
