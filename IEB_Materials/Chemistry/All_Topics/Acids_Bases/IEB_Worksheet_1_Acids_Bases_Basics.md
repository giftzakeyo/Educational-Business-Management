### **Section B: Definitions (5 marks)**
1. **Acid:** Proton (H⁺) donor (1)
2. **Conjugate pair:** Acid and base related by gain/loss of one H⁺ (1)
3. **pH:** -log₁₀[H⁺], measure of acidity (1)
4. **Strong acid:** Completely dissociates in water (1)
5. **Ka:** Equilibrium constant for acid dissociation, Ka = [H⁺][A⁻]/[HA] (1)

### **Section C: Calculations (10 marks)**
1. [H⁺] = 0.001 M, pH = -log(0.001) = 3 (2)
2. [OH⁻] = 0.01 M, pOH = -log(0.01) = 2, pH = 14 - 2 = 12 (2)
3. pH = -log(2.5×10⁻⁴) = 3.60, [OH⁻] = 1.0×10⁻¹⁴/2.5×10⁻⁴ = 4.0×10⁻¹¹ M (2)
4. pKa = -log(4.0×10⁻⁶) = 5.40 (2)
5. Kb = 1.0×10⁻¹⁴/1.8×10⁻⁵ = 5.56×10⁻¹⁰ (2)

### **Section D: Conjugate Pairs (5 marks)**
1. Acid: HCl, conjugate base: Cl⁻; Base: NH₃, conjugate acid: NH₄⁺ (1)
2. Ka = [H⁺][CH₃COO⁻]/[CH₃COOH] (1)
3. Kb = [NH₄⁺][OH⁻]/[NH₃] (1)
4. H₂SO₄ + H₂O → H₃O⁺ + HSO₄⁻ (1)
5. H₂O + H₂O ⇌ H₃O⁺ + OH⁻ or H₂O ⇌ H⁺ + OH⁻ (1)
