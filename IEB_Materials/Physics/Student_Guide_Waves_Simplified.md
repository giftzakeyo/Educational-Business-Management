# 🌊 Grade 12 Waves - SIMPLIFIED GUIDE

## **Part 1: What ARE Waves?**

### **Simple Definition:**
A wave is a **disturbance that carries energy from one place to another WITHOUT carrying matter**.

**Think of it like this:**
- Throw a stone in water → ripples spread out
- The water doesn't travel with the ripples
- Only the ENERGY travels

---

## **Part 2: Two Main Types of Waves**

### **1. Mechanical Waves** - Need a "medium" (stuff) to travel through
**Examples:**
- Sound waves (air, water, solids)
- Water waves (water)
- Seismic waves (earth)
- Waves on a rope

### **2. Electromagnetic Waves** - Can travel through EMPTY SPACE
**Examples:**
- Light waves
- Radio waves
- X-rays
- Microwaves

**Memory Trick:** "Mr. X-Ray Loves Radio" = Microwave, X-ray, Light, Radio

---

## **Part 3: How Waves Move - Two Patterns**

### **Transverse Waves** - Like a snake
- Particles move UP and DOWN
- Wave travels SIDEWAYS
- **Examples:** Light waves, water waves, waves on a rope

```
UP ↑    Crest (top)
        |     |
        |     |
DOWN ↓  Trough (bottom)
```

### **Longitudinal Waves** - Like a slinky
- Particles move BACK and FORTH
- Wave travels in SAME direction
- **Examples:** Sound waves, seismic P-waves

```
Compression (squeezed) ←→ Rarefaction (spread out)
```

---

## **Part 4: Wave Properties - The ABCs of Waves**

### **A = Amplitude**
- **What:** Height of the wave
- **Means:** How much ENERGY the wave has
- **Big amplitude =** Loud sound (sound) or Bright light (light)

### **B = Wavelength (λ - "lambda")**
- **What:** Distance from one crest to the next crest
- **Measures:** How LONG the wave is
- **Short wavelength =** High frequency (more waves in same space)

### **C = Frequency (f)**
- **What:** How many waves pass a point in 1 second
- **Unit:** Hertz (Hz) = waves per second
- **High frequency =** High pitch (sound) or Blue color (light)

### **D = Period (T)**
- **What:** Time for ONE complete wave
- **Formula:** T = 1/f (if f=10Hz, T=0.1s)
- **Short period =** High frequency

### **E = Wave Speed (v)**
- **What:** How fast the wave travels
- **Formula:** v = f × λ (THE MOST IMPORTANT FORMULA!)

---

## **Part 5: The Magic Formula - v = f × λ**

### **What it means:**
Speed = Frequency × Wavelength

### **How to use it:**
1. If you know ANY TWO, you can find the third
2. **Example:** Radio wave: f = 100 MHz, λ = 3 m
   v = 100,000,000 × 3 = 300,000,000 m/s (speed of light!)

### **Triangle Method:**
```
     v
    ┌───┐
 f  │   │  λ
    └───┘
Cover what you want to find:
- Cover v → f × λ
- Cover f → v ÷ λ  
- Cover λ → v ÷ f
```

---

## **Part 6: Wave Behaviors - What Waves DO**

### **1. Reflection - The Bounce**
- Wave hits a surface and bounces back
- **Example:** Echo (sound), Mirror (light)
- **Rule:** Angle in = Angle out

### **2. Refraction - The Bend**
- Wave changes speed and bends when entering new material
- **Example:** Pencil looks bent in water
- **Why:** Light slows down in water

### **3. Diffraction - The Spread**
- Wave bends around corners or through openings
- **Example:** Hearing music around a corner
- **Works best when:** Opening size ≈ Wavelength

### **4. Interference - The Mix**
- Two waves meet and combine
- **Constructive:** Waves add up (louder/brighter)
- **Destructive:** Waves cancel out (softer/darker)

---

## **Part 7: Sound Waves - What You HEAR**

### **Three Properties of Sound:**

#### **1. Pitch → Frequency**
- High frequency = High pitch (whistle)
- Low frequency = Low pitch (drum)

#### **2. Loudness → Amplitude**
- Big amplitude = Loud sound
- Small amplitude = Soft sound

#### **3. Quality → Wave Shape**
- Different instruments, same note
- Makes violin sound different from piano

### **Speed of Sound:**
- Air: ~340 m/s (depends on temperature)
- Water: ~1500 m/s (faster!)
- Steel: ~5000 m/s (fastest!)

---

## **Part 8: Light Waves - What You SEE**

### **Electromagnetic Spectrum:**
**From LONG wavelength to SHORT:**

1. **Radio waves** - Longest, carry music/TV
2. **Microwaves** - Heat food, cell phones
3. **Infrared** - Heat cameras, remote controls
4. **Visible light** - What we see (ROYGBIV)
5. **Ultraviolet** - Sun tan, black lights
6. **X-rays** - See bones, airport security
7. **Gamma rays** - Shortest, most energy, cancer treatment

**Memory:** "Rabbits Mate In Very Unusual eXpensive Gardens"

### **Speed of Light:**
- Always: 300,000,000 m/s (in vacuum)
- Symbol: c
- Fastest possible speed in universe!

---

## **Part 9: Important Formulas - CHEAT SHEET**

### **Must Know:**
1. **v = f × λ** (Wave speed = frequency × wavelength)
2. **T = 1/f** (Period = 1 ÷ frequency)
3. **f = 1/T** (Frequency = 1 ÷ period)

### **Unit Conversions:**
- 1 kHz = 1000 Hz
- 1 MHz = 1,000,000 Hz
- 1 m = 100 cm = 1000 mm
- 1 nm = 0.000000001 m (10⁻⁹ m)

### **Special Speeds:**
- Speed of light: c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
- Speed of sound in air: ~340 m/s (use 340 for calculations)

---

## **Part 10: Solving Problems - STEP BY STEP**

### **Example Problem:**
A sound wave has frequency 500 Hz and wavelength 0.68 m. Find its speed.

**Step 1:** Write what you know
- f = 500 Hz
- λ = 0.68 m
- v = ? (what we want)

**Step 2:** Choose the right formula
- v = f × λ

**Step 3:** Substitute numbers
- v = 500 × 0.68

**Step 4:** Calculate
- v = 340 m/s

**Step 5:** Check units and reasonableness
- m/s is correct for speed
- 340 m/s is reasonable for sound in air ✓

## **Part 11: Common Exam Questions**

### **Type 1: Calculations (v = fλ)**
**Question:** Light wave has λ = 600 nm, find f.
**Solution:** 
1. Convert nm to m: 600 nm = 600 × 10⁻⁹ = 6 × 10⁻⁷ m
2. v = c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
3. f = v/λ = (3 × 10⁸)/(6 × 10⁻⁷) = 5 × 10¹⁴ Hz

### **Type 2: Explanations**
**Question:** Why does a pencil look bent in water?
**Answer:** Light refracts (bends) when moving from air to water because it slows down.

### **Type 3: Comparisons**
**Question:** Compare radio waves and gamma rays.
**Answer:** Radio: long λ, low f, low energy. Gamma: short λ, high f, high energy.

## **Part 14: Common Mistakes to AVOID**

### **Mistake 1:** Confusing wavelength and amplitude
- **Wavelength:** Distance crest to crest
- **Amplitude:** Height from middle to crest

### **Mistake 2:** Forgetting to convert units
- nm → m (divide by 10⁹)
- kHz → Hz (multiply by 1000)

### **Mistake 3:** Mixing up transverse and longitudinal
- **Transverse:** Up/down movement (light)
- **Longitudinal:** Back/forth movement (sound)

### **Mistake 4:** Using wrong speed
- **Light:** Always 3×10⁸ m/s (in vacuum)
- **Sound:** ~340 m/s in air (changes with temperature)

## **Part 15: Quick Self-Test**

### **True or False:**
1. Waves carry matter from one place to another. **FALSE**
2. Light waves are transverse. **TRUE**
3. High frequency means long wavelength. **FALSE**
4. Sound travels faster in water than air. **TRUE**
5. All electromagnetic waves travel at same speed in vacuum. **TRUE**

### **Fill in the blanks:**
1. Distance between crests = __________ **(wavelength)**
2. Number of waves per second = __________ **(frequency)**
3. v = __ × λ **(f)**
4. Speed of light = __________ m/s **(3×10⁸)**
5. Unit of frequency = __________ **(Hertz)**

### **Calculation:**
A wave has f = 50 Hz, λ = 4 m. Find v.
**Answer:** v = 50 × 4 = 200 m/s
