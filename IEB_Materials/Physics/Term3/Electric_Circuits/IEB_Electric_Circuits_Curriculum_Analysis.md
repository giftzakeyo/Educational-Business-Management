# IEB Electric Circuits - Curriculum Analysis (Grade 12 Physics)

## IEB CAPS Requirements

### What IEB Expects Students To Know:

#### 1. Ohm's Law (Revision from Grade 11)
- State Ohm's Law: The current through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference across it, provided temperature remains constant
- R = V/I (definition of resistance)
- Factors affecting resistance: length, cross-sectional area, temperature, material type
- Ohm's Law experiment: circuit diagram, measurements, graph of V vs I

#### 2. Internal Resistance (NEW for Grade 12)
- **Emf (ε):** The total energy supplied per coulomb of charge by the cell (measured in volts)
- **Terminal potential difference (V):** The voltage across the terminals of a cell when current is flowing
- **Internal resistance (r):** The resistance inside a cell/battery due to the electrolyte and electrodes
- **Relationship:** ε = V + Ir  OR  ε = I(R + r)
- When current increases, terminal voltage DECREASES (due to greater voltage drop across internal resistance)
- A cell's emf is measured when NO current flows (voltmeter across open terminals)

#### 3. Series and Parallel Circuits
- **Series:** Current same everywhere, voltage divides, R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ...
- **Parallel:** Voltage same across branches, current divides, 1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ...
- Combination circuits (series-parallel)

#### 4. Electrical Power and Energy
- **Power:** P = VI = I²R = V²/R
- **Energy:** E = P × t = VIt
- Kilowatt-hours (kWh) as a unit of energy
- Cost of electricity calculations

### IEB Exam Weighting
- Electric Circuits typically appears in **Paper 1**
- Usually worth **15-25 marks** (multiple choice + longer questions)
- Often combined with Electrochemistry connections
- Internal resistance questions are **high-frequency** in IEB Paper 1

### Common IEB Exam Question Types:
1. Calculate total resistance in combination circuits
2. Find current and voltage readings on meters
3. Calculate internal resistance using ε = I(R + r)
4. Power calculations and cost of electricity
5. Graph interpretation (V vs I to find emf and r)
6. Explain observations (e.g., "Why does the bulb dim when more devices are connected?")

### Key IEB Skills Assessed:
- Drawing circuit diagrams with proper symbols
- Solving simultaneous equations from circuit networks
- Interpreting V-I graphs (y-intercept = ε, gradient = -r)
- Experimental design and evaluation
