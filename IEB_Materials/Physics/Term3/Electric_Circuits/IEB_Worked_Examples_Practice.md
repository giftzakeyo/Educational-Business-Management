## Worked Example 3: Combination Circuits (IEB Favourite)

```
          ┌─── 6Ω ───┐
          │          │
    ──────┤          ├──────
          │          │
          └─── 3Ω ───┘
          │          │
          └─── R ────┘
          
          V = 18 V
```

In the circuit above, a 6 Ω resistor, a 3 Ω resistor, and an unknown resistor R are connected in parallel across an 18 V battery. The total current drawn from the battery is 12 A.

**3.1** Calculate the current through the 6 Ω resistor. (3)

**3.2** Calculate the current through the 3 Ω resistor. (3)

**3.3** Determine the value of resistor R. (4)

## Practice Activity 2: Series-Parallel Circuit

```
          ┌─── 4Ω ────┐
          │           │
    ──────┤           ├──────
          │           │
          └─── 6Ω ────┘
          │           │
          ───── 3Ω ────
          
          V = 12 V
```

In the circuit above, a 4 Ω and 6 Ω resistor are connected in parallel. A 3 Ω resistor is in series with the parallel combination. The battery is 12 V.

**P2.1** Calculate the total resistance of the circuit. (4)

**P2.2** Calculate the total current. (3)

**P2.3** Calculate the voltage across the 3 Ω resistor. (3)

**P2.4** Calculate the current through the 4 Ω resistor. (3)

**P2.5** Calculate the power dissipated in the 6 Ω resistor. (4)

## Practice Activity 3: V vs I Graph (IEB Classic)

In an experiment, a student measures the terminal voltage of a battery for different currents. The results are:

| Current (A) | 0.0 | 0.5 | 1.0 | 1.5 | 2.0 | 2.5 |
|-------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Voltage (V) | 9.0 | 8.5 | 8.0 | 7.5 | 7.0 | 6.5 |

**P3.1** Plot a graph of voltage (y-axis) vs current (x-axis). (4)

**P3.2** Use the graph to determine the emf of the battery. (2)

**P3.3** Calculate the gradient of the graph. (3)

**P3.4** What physical quantity does the gradient represent? (2)

**P3.5** Use your answers to write the equation relating V and I for this battery. (3)

**P3.6** Calculate the short-circuit current of this battery. (3)
