## Key Formulas Reference Box

| Formula | What it gives | When to use |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| E = hf | Photon energy | Given frequency, find energy |
| E = hc/λ | Photon energy | Given wavelength, find energy |
| W₀ = hf₀ | Work function | At threshold frequency |
| E_k = hf - W₀ | Max KE of electron | Photoelectric effect |
| E_k = ½mv² | Max KE (from velocity) | Find electron speed |
| 1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ J | Energy conversion | Convert between units |

## Worked Example 3: Photoelectric Effect Graph (IEB Favourite)

In a photoelectric effect experiment, the maximum kinetic energy of ejected electrons is measured for different frequencies of incident light. The results are:

| Frequency (×10¹⁴ Hz) | 4.0 | 5.0 | 6.0 | 7.0 | 8.0 | 9.0 |
|----------------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| E_k(max) (×10⁻¹⁹ J) | 0.0 | 0.7 | 2.0 | 3.3 | 4.6 | 5.9 |

**3.1** Plot a graph of E_k(max) (y-axis) vs frequency (x-axis). (4)

**3.2** Use the graph to determine the threshold frequency of the metal. (2)

**3.3** Calculate the gradient of the graph. (3)

**3.4** What does the gradient represent? Explain. (2)

**3.5** Determine the work function of the metal. (3)

**3.6** Write down Einstein's photoelectric equation using your values. (2)
