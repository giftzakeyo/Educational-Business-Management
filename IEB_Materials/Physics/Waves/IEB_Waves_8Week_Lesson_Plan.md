# IEB Physical Sciences: 8-Week Waves Lesson Plan (Grade 12)

## **Complete Teaching Schedule with Daily Activities, Assessments, and Resources**

---

## **Week 1: Foundation & Wave Properties**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Review Grade 10-11 wave concepts
- Define and calculate wave properties
- Distinguish between transverse and longitudinal waves
- Apply v = fλ to solve problems

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 1: Introduction & Diagnostic**
- **Starter:** "What do you remember about waves?" mind map
- **Content:** Review wave types (mechanical/EM, transverse/longitudinal)
- **Activity:** Wave charades - act out different wave types
- **Assessment:** Quick quiz (5 questions)
- **Homework:** Definitions worksheet

#### **Day 2: Wave Properties**
- **Starter:** Label wave diagram on board
- **Content:** Amplitude, wavelength, frequency, period, speed
- **Formula:** v = fλ introduction
- **Activity:** Measuring waves on slinky/rope
- **Practice:** Basic calculations
- **Homework:** Calculation worksheet 1

#### **Day 3: Calculations Practice**
- **Starter:** Unit conversion practice (Hz ↔ kHz ↔ MHz)
- **Content:** Solving v = fλ problems
- **Differentiation:** Tiered worksheets (basic → advanced)
- **Activity:** Problem-solving stations
- **Assessment:** In-class quiz (10 marks)
- **Homework:** Past paper questions

#### **Day 4: Real-World Applications**
- **Starter:** "Where do we encounter waves daily?"
- **Content:** Sound waves, seismic waves, water waves
- **Activity:** Research and present one wave application
- **Discussion:** Social impact of wave technologies
- **Homework:** Application research

#### **Day 5: Review & Consolidation**
- **Starter:** Concept matching game
- **Activity:** Group problem-solving challenge
- **Review:** Common mistakes and how to avoid them
- **Assessment:** Weekly test (20 marks)
- **Reflection:** "What was challenging this week?"

---

## **Week 2: Wave Behaviors**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Describe and explain reflection, refraction, diffraction, interference
- Apply the principle of superposition
- Understand standing waves and resonance
- Conduct practical investigations

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 6: Reflection & Refraction**
- **Starter:** Pencil in water demonstration
- **Content:** Laws of reflection and refraction
- **Demo:** Ripple tank demonstrations
- **Activity:** Drawing ray diagrams
- **Homework:** Reflection/refraction worksheet

#### **Day 7: Diffraction & Interference**
- **Starter:** Hearing around corners discussion
- **Content:** Diffraction conditions, interference patterns
- **Demo:** Two-source interference in ripple tank
- **Activity:** Young's double slit calculations
- **Homework:** Interference problems

#### **Day 8: Standing Waves & Resonance**
- **Starter:** Singing wine glass demo
- **Content:** Nodes, antinodes, harmonics
- **Activity:** Standing waves on string/slinky
- **Practical:** Measuring fundamental frequency
- **Homework:** Standing wave calculations

#### **Day 9: Practical Investigation**
- **Title:** "Investigating Wave Speed on a String"
- **Duration:** Double period (2 hours)
- **Assessment:** Lab report (20 marks)
- **Skills:** Planning, execution, analysis, conclusion
- **Extension:** Error analysis and improvements

#### **Day 10: Behavior Integration**
- **Starter:** Identify behaviors in video clips
- **Activity:** "Wave behavior scavenger hunt" - find examples around school
- **Review:** Concept maps linking all behaviors
- **Assessment:** Explanation questions
- **Homework:** Revision for test

---

## **Week 3: Sound Waves & Doppler Effect**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Understand sound wave characteristics
- Calculate speed of sound in different media
- Apply Doppler effect formula
- Explain ultrasound applications

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 11: Sound Properties**
- **Starter:** Different instrument sounds
- **Content:** Pitch (frequency), loudness (amplitude), quality
- **Demo:** Oscilloscope sound waves
- **Activity:** Measuring speed of sound experiment
- **Homework:** Sound calculations

#### **Day 12: Speed of Sound**
- **Starter:** "Why see lightning before thunder?"
- **Content:** Speed in different media (solids > liquids > gases)
- **Formula:** v = √(B/ρ) qualitative understanding
- **Activity:** Echo calculations
- **Homework:** Echo problems

#### **Day 13: Doppler Effect Introduction**
- **Starter:** Ambulance siren video
- **Content:** Qualitative explanation
- **Demo:** Doppler ball (buzzer on string)
- **Activity:** Predicting frequency changes
- **Homework:** Qualitative questions

#### **Day 14: Doppler Effect Calculations**
- **Starter:** Formula derivation
- **Content:** f' = f(v ± v₀)/(v ∓ vₛ)
- **Practice:** Step-by-step problems
- **Differentiation:** Scaffolded worksheets
- **Homework:** Doppler calculation worksheet

#### **Day 15: Ultrasound & Applications**
- **Starter:** Baby ultrasound image
- **Content:** Medical and industrial applications
- **Activity:** Research project on ultrasound uses
- **Discussion:** Ethics of prenatal ultrasound
- **Assessment:** Application questions (15 marks)

---

## **Week 4: Light Waves & EM Spectrum**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Describe electromagnetic waves and spectrum
- Explain polarization of light
- Apply wave concepts to light
- Understand technological applications

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 16: Electromagnetic Waves**
- **Starter:** "What do radio, light, and X-rays have in common?"
- **Content:** EM wave properties, speed of light
- **Activity:** EM spectrum sorting game
- **Homework:** Spectrum worksheet

#### **Day 17: Light Behaviors**
- **Starter:** Rainbow formation discussion
- **Content:** Reflection, refraction, total internal reflection
- **Demo:** Prism, fiber optics
- **Activity:** Snell's Law calculations
- **Homework:** Refraction problems

#### **Day 18: Diffraction & Interference of Light**
- **Starter:** CD rainbow patterns
- **Content:** Young's experiment, diffraction grating
- **Demo:** Laser through slits
- **Activity:** Calculating wavelength from fringe spacing
- **Homework:** Interference calculations

#### **Day 19: Polarization**
- **Starter:** Sunglasses demonstration
- **Content:** Polarization concept and applications
- **Activity:** Using polarizers to analyze light
- **Practical:** Malus' Law investigation
- **Homework:** Polarization questions

#### **Day 20: Technology Applications**
- **Starter:** "How does your phone use waves?"
- **Content:** Communication, medical, domestic applications
- **Activity:** Group presentations on technologies
- **Assessment:** Technology essay (20 marks)
- **Homework:** Revision for mid-unit test

---

## **Week 5: Advanced Concepts & Integration**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Solve complex integrated problems
- Apply multiple concepts to real scenarios
- Develop exam technique
- Conduct revision activities

### **Daily Breakdown:**

#### **Day 21: Complex Problem Solving**
- **Starter:** Multi-step problem example
- **Content:** Integrating Doppler, interference, calculations
- **Strategy:** Problem decomposition method
- **Activity:** Worked examples then practice
- **Homework:** Integrated problems

#### **Day 22: Exam Technique Workshop**
- **Starter:** Common exam mistakes
- **Content:** Reading questions, showing working, managing time
- **Activity:** Marking sample answers
- **Practice:** Under exam conditions
- **Homework:** Past paper section

#### **Day 23: Practical Skills Development**
- **Title:** "Determining Wavelength Using Diffraction Grating"
- **Duration:** Double period
- **Assessment:** Practical skills checklist
- **Focus:** Accuracy, precision, error analysis
- **Extension:** Comparing methods

#### **Day 24: Revision Stations**
- **Setup:** 5 stations (15 min each)
  1. Calculations practice
  2. Definitions and explanations
  3. Diagram drawing
  4. Application questions
  5. Peer teaching
- **Activity:** Rotate through stations
- **Homework:** Comprehensive revision

#### **Day 25: Mid-Unit Test**
- **Duration:** 1 hour
- **Marks:** 50
- **Format:** Multiple choice, calculations, explanations
- **Content:** Weeks 1-4
- **After test:** Feedback and improvement planning

- Peer tutoring
- Formula sheets with explanations

### **For Advanced Students:**
- Extension problems with real data
- Research projects on advanced topics
- Leadership roles in group work
- Participation in science competitions

### **For EAL Students:**
- Bilingual vocabulary lists

- Sentence frames for explanations

- Additional time for reading tasks
