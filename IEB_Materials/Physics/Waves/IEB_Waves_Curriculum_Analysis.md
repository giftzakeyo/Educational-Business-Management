# IEB Physical Sciences: Waves Curriculum Analysis & Alignment

## **Based on: IEB Physical Sciences Curriculum and Assessment Manual 2024**

---

## **Part 1: IEB Curriculum Requirements for Waves**

### **Grade 10-12 Progression:**

#### **Grade 10 (Introduction):**
- **Topic:** Wave motion
- **Content:** 
  - Transverse and longitudinal waves
  - Amplitude, wavelength, frequency, period, wave speed
  - Relationship: v = fλ
  - Sound as longitudinal wave
  - Pitch and loudness
  - Electromagnetic spectrum (basic)

#### **Grade 11 (Development):**
- **Topic:** Sound and light
- **Content:**
  - Speed of sound in different media
  - Ultrasound and applications
  - Reflection, refraction, diffraction, interference
  - Standing waves and resonance
  - Light as electromagnetic wave
  - Colour and frequency relationship

#### **Grade 12 (Advanced):**
- **Topic:** Wave phenomena
- **Content:**
  - Doppler effect (qualitative and quantitative)
  - Photoelectric effect (wave-particle duality)
  - Diffraction grating
  - Polarization
  - Applications in technology and medicine

## **Part 3: Specific Learning Outcomes for Waves**

### **Students must be able to:**

#### **Conceptual Understanding:**
1. Distinguish between transverse and longitudinal waves
2. Explain wave properties using appropriate terminology
3. Describe wave behaviors (reflection, refraction, etc.)
4. Relate wave concepts to real-world applications

#### **Mathematical Skills:**
1. Use v = fλ to solve problems
2. Calculate frequency, wavelength, speed, period
3. Apply Doppler effect formula: f' = f(v ± v₀)/(v ∓ vₛ)
4. Convert between units (Hz, kHz, MHz; m, cm, nm)

#### **Practical Skills:**
1. Design experiments to measure wave properties
2. Use apparatus safely (ripple tank, tuning forks, etc.)
3. Record and analyze data accurately
4. Draw conclusions from experimental results

#### **Application Skills:**
1. Explain technological applications of waves
2. Analyze wave phenomena in everyday life
3. Solve problems in context (medical, communication, etc.)
4. Evaluate the impact of wave technology on society

## **Part 6: Common IEB Exam Questions**

### **Type A: Multiple Choice (Knowledge)**
**Example:** Which wave property determines loudness?
A) Frequency  
B) Wavelength  
C) Amplitude  
D) Speed  
**Answer:** C

### **Type B: Definitions (Comprehension)**
**Example:** Define the term "frequency" in the context of wave motion.  
**Answer:** Number of complete waves passing a point per second, measured in Hertz (Hz).

### **Type C: Calculations (Application)**
**Example:** A sound wave has frequency 500 Hz and speed 340 m/s. Calculate its wavelength.  
**Solution:** λ = v/f = 340/500 = 0.68 m

### **Type D: Explanations (Analysis)**
**Example:** Explain why a pencil appears bent when placed in water.  
**Answer:** Light refracts when moving from air to water due to change in speed, causing bending.

### **Type E: Applications (Evaluation)**
**Example:** Discuss two medical applications of ultrasound waves.  
**Answer:** 1) Imaging of internal organs and fetuses. 2) Breaking kidney stones (lithotripsy).

## **Part 10: Sample Lesson Plan (IEB Grade 12)**

### **Lesson: Doppler Effect**
**Duration:** 2 hours (double period)

#### **Learning Objectives:**
- State the Doppler effect
- Explain why frequency changes with relative motion
- Solve Doppler effect problems
- Apply to real-world situations

#### **Resources:**
- Video of passing ambulance
- Doppler ball demonstration
- Worksheets with problems
- Calculator

#### **Lesson Structure:**

**Starter (10 min):**
- Video: Ambulance siren passing
- Question: "Why does the pitch change?"

**Direct Instruction (20 min):**
- Define Doppler effect
- Explain using wavefront diagrams
- Introduce formula: f' = f(v ± v₀)/(v ∓ vₛ)

**Demonstration (15 min):**
- Doppler ball (buzzer on string)
- Students observe frequency change

**Guided Practice (25 min):**
- Work through examples on board
- Step-by-step problem solving
- Common mistakes highlighted

**Independent Practice (30 min):**
- Worksheet with 5 problems
- Include real-world contexts
- Peer checking

**Plenary (10 min):**
- Applications: radar, astronomy (redshift)
- Exit ticket: "Explain Doppler effect to a friend"

#### **Homework:**
- Textbook problems on Doppler effect
- Research: How is Doppler used in weather forecasting?

#### **Assessment:**
- Formative: Worksheet completion
- Summative: Test question next week

## **Part 11: IEB Examination Preparation**

### **Study Guide for Students:**

#### **Must Know Formulas:**
1. v = fλ (wave equation)
2. T = 1/f (period-frequency relationship)
3. f' = f(v ± v₀)/(v ∓ vₛ) (Doppler effect)
4. sinθ₁/sinθ₂ = v₁/v₂ = n₂/n₁ (Snell's Law)

#### **Key Concepts to Master:**
1. Wave types and properties
2. Wave behaviors (reflection, refraction, etc.)
3. Sound characteristics and applications
4. Light as electromagnetic wave
5. Modern applications and technology

#### **Exam Technique:**
1. **Read carefully:** Underline key words
2. **Show working:** Get method marks even if answer wrong
3. **Check units:** Always include correct units
4. **Manage time:** Don't spend too long on one question
5. **Review:** Check calculations and answers

#### **Past Paper Strategy:**
1. Complete 3-5 past papers under timed conditions
2. Mark using memorandum
3. Identify weak areas
4. Focus revision on those topics
5. Repeat until confident
