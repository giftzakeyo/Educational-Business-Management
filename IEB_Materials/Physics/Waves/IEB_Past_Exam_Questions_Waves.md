## **Question 1: Multiple Choice (IEB 2023)**

**A sound wave travels from air into water. Which ONE of the following remains constant?**

A) Wavelength  
B) Frequency  
C) Speed  
D) Amplitude  

**Answer:** B) Frequency  
**Explanation:** When a wave crosses a boundary between media, its frequency remains constant. The speed and wavelength change.

## **Question 2: Calculation (IEB 2022)**

**A ship uses sonar to determine the depth of the ocean floor. A sound pulse is emitted and the echo is detected 0.8 seconds later. If the speed of sound in seawater is 1500 m/s, calculate the depth of the ocean at that point.**

**Solution:**
- Time for sound to travel to bottom and back = 0.8 s
- Time to bottom only = 0.8 ÷ 2 = 0.4 s
- Distance = speed × time = 1500 × 0.4 = 600 m

**Answer:** 600 m

---

## **Question 3: Explanation (IEB 2021)**

**Explain why different musical instruments playing the same note sound different.**

**Answer:**
- Same note means same fundamental frequency (pitch)
- Different instruments produce different harmonics (overtones)
- Waveform shape (timbre) is different
- This gives each instrument its unique quality or tone color

**Answer:**

## **Question 5: Doppler Effect (IEB 2019)**

**An ambulance with its siren on approaches a stationary observer at 30 m/s. The siren emits sound of frequency 800 Hz. The speed of sound in air is 340 m/s.**

**Calculate the frequency heard by the observer.**

**Solution:**
- Source moving toward observer: f' = f × v/(v - vₛ)
- f' = 800 × 340/(340 - 30)
- f' = 800 × 340/310
- f' = 800 × 1.097
- f' = 877.4 Hz

**Answer:** 877 Hz

## **Question 6: Application (IEB 2018)**

**Describe TWO medical applications of ultrasound waves.**

**Answer:**
1. **Diagnostic imaging:** Ultrasound scans create images of internal organs, fetuses, and blood flow by analyzing echoes from different tissues.
2. **Therapeutic treatment:** High-intensity focused ultrasound (HIFU) can destroy tumors or kidney stones without surgery.

## **Question 7: Wave Properties (IEB 2017)**

**A wave has the following characteristics:**
- Amplitude: 0.2 m
- Frequency: 5 Hz
- Wavelength: 0.6 m

**Calculate:**
a) The period of the wave  
b) The speed of the wave

**Solution:**
a) T = 1/f = 1/5 = 0.2 s  
b) v = fλ = 5 × 0.6 = 3 m/s

**Answer:** a) 0.2 s, b) 3 m/s

## **Question 9: Standing Waves (IEB 2015)**

**A guitar string of length 0.65 m vibrates at its fundamental frequency. The speed of waves on the string is 420 m/s.**

**Calculate the fundamental frequency.**

**Solution:**
- For fundamental, λ = 2L = 2 × 0.65 = 1.3 m
- f = v/λ = 420/1.3 = 323.1 Hz

**Answer:** 323 Hz

## **Question 10: Refraction (IEB 2014)**

**Light travels from air (n=1.00) into glass (n=1.50).**

**a) Does the light speed up or slow down?**  
**b) Does it bend toward or away from the normal?**

**Answer:**
a) Slows down (higher refractive index = slower speed)  
b) Toward the normal (when entering denser medium)

## **Question 11: Practical Investigation (IEB 2013)**

**Design an experiment to measure the speed of sound in air using a simple method.**

**Answer:**
**Apparatus:** Two blocks of wood, stopwatch, measuring tape  
**Method:**
1. Stand a known distance (e.g., 100 m) from a wall
2. Clap two blocks together sharply
3. Start stopwatch at clap, stop when echo heard
4. Repeat several times, calculate average time
5. Speed = 2 × distance ÷ time (sound travels to wall and back)

**Precautions:** Do on calm day, avoid background noise, use large distance for accuracy

## **Question 12: Wave Behavior (IEB 2012)**

**Explain the difference between reflection and refraction of waves.**

**Answer:**
- **Reflection:** Wave bounces off a surface. Angle of incidence = angle of reflection. Wave stays in same medium.
- **Refraction:** Wave bends when entering new medium. Caused by change in speed. Wavelength changes but frequency constant.

## **Question 13: Calculations with Units (IEB 2011)**

**A radio wave has frequency 95.8 MHz and travels at 3 × 10⁸ m/s.**

**a) Convert frequency to Hz**  
**b) Calculate wavelength in meters**  
**c) Express wavelength in cm**

**Solution:**
a) 95.8 MHz = 95.8 × 10⁶ = 9.58 × 10⁷ Hz  
b) λ = v/f = 3×10⁸/9.58×10⁷ = 3.13 m  
c) 3.13 m = 313 cm

**Answer:** a) 9.58×10⁷ Hz, b) 3.13 m, c) 313 cm

## **Question 14: Real-World Context (IEB 2010)**

**Why are radio waves used for long-distance communication rather than visible light?**

**Answer:**
- Radio waves have longer wavelengths
- They diffract around obstacles (hills, buildings)
- They can follow Earth's curvature
- Less affected by atmospheric conditions
- Can penetrate buildings better

a) Type of wave (transverse/longitudinal)

**Answer:**

## **Question 17: Problem-Solving (IEB 2007)**

**Two waves interfere. Wave A has amplitude 3 cm, Wave B has amplitude 4 cm. What are the maximum and minimum possible amplitudes when they interfere?**

**Solution:**
- Constructive interference (in phase): A_max = 3 + 4 = 7 cm
- Destructive interference (out of phase): A_min = |4 - 3| = 1 cm

**Answer:** Maximum 7 cm, Minimum 1 cm

---

## **Question 18: Technology Application (IEB 2006)**

**Explain how a microwave oven heats food.**

**Answer:**
- Microwaves (frequency ~2.45 GHz) are emitted
- Water molecules in food are polar and try to align with alternating electric field
- Rapid molecular rotation creates friction and heat
- Heat spreads through food by conduction

## **Question 19: Calculations with Scientific Notation (IEB 2005)**

**Blue light has wavelength 450 nm. Calculate its frequency.**
(Speed of light = 3.00 × 10⁸ m/s)

**Solution:**
- λ = 450 nm = 450 × 10⁻⁹ = 4.50 × 10⁻⁷ m
- f = v/λ = 3.00×10⁸/4.50×10⁻⁷ = 6.67 × 10¹⁴ Hz

**Answer:** 6.67 × 10¹⁴ Hz

| Frequency (Hz) | Wavelength (m) |
|----------------|----------------|

## **Exam Technique Tips:**

### **Before the Exam:**
1. **Know your formulas:** v = fλ, T = 1/f, Doppler formula
2. **Practice unit conversions:** Hz ↔ kHz ↔ MHz, m ↔ cm ↔ nm
3. **Understand concepts:** Don't just memorize - understand why

### **During the Exam:**
1. **Read carefully:** Underline key words in questions
2. **Show working:** Get method marks even if final answer wrong
3. **Check units:** Always include correct units in answers
4. **Manage time:** Don't spend too long on one question
5. **Review:** Check calculations before submitting

### **Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Confusing wavelength and amplitude
- Forgetting to convert units (nm to m, kHz to Hz)
- Using wrong speed (sound vs light)
- Not showing working in calculations
- Misapplying Doppler effect formula signs

## **Formula Sheet (Memorize These):**

### **Essential Formulas:**
1. **Wave equation:** v = f × λ
2. **Period-frequency:** T = 1/f or f = 1/T
3. **Doppler effect:** f' = f(v ± v₀)/(v ∓ vₛ)
4. **Speed of light:** c = 3.00 × 10⁸ m/s
5. **Speed of sound in air:** ~340 m/s

### **Unit Conversions:**
- 1 kHz = 1000 Hz
- 1 MHz = 1,000,000 Hz
- 1 nm = 10⁻⁹ m
- 1 cm = 0.01 m

### **Special Values:**
- Speed of light: 3 × 10⁸ m/s
- Speed of sound: 340 m/s (use unless told otherwise)
- Human hearing range: 20 Hz - 20,000 Hz
