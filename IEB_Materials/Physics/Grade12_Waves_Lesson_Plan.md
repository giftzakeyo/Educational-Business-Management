# Grade 12 Physics: Waves Lesson Plan

## **Subject:** Physics
## **Grade Level:** 12
## **Duration:** 4-5 weeks (15-20 class periods)
## **Prerequisites:** Grade 11 Physics, basic trigonometry, understanding of motion

---

## **Unit Overview**

This unit covers wave phenomena with emphasis on mathematical analysis, real-world applications, and preparation for final examinations. The content aligns with CAPS (South African Curriculum) and international physics standards.

---

## **Week 1: Introduction to Waves & Wave Properties**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Define waves and distinguish between mechanical and electromagnetic waves
- Differentiate between transverse and longitudinal waves
- Identify and calculate wave properties: amplitude, wavelength, frequency, period, speed
- Apply the wave equation: v = fλ

### **Daily Lesson Breakdown:**

#### **Day 1: What are Waves?**
- **Hook:** Show videos of ocean waves, sound waves (oscilloscope), light waves
- **Key Concepts:**
  - Wave definition: disturbance that transfers energy without transferring matter
  - Mechanical vs. electromagnetic waves
  - Medium requirement
- **Activity:** "Wave Charades" - act out different wave types

#### **Day 2: Transverse vs. Longitudinal Waves**
- **Demonstrations:**
  - Slinky for transverse and longitudinal waves
  - Rope waves (transverse)
  - Sound in air (longitudinal - use tuning fork in water)
- **Diagrams:** Particle motion vs. wave propagation
- **Worksheet:** Labeling wave types in various scenarios

#### **Day 3: Wave Properties**
- **Definitions & Symbols:**
  - Amplitude (A) - maximum displacement from equilibrium
  - Wavelength (λ) - distance between successive crests
  - Frequency (f) - number of waves per second (Hz)
  - Period (T) - time for one complete wave (T = 1/f)
  - Wave speed (v) - speed of wave propagation
- **Formula:** v = fλ (derivation and application)

#### **Day 4: Calculations Practice**
- **Problem Types:**
  1. Given f and λ, find v
  2. Given v and f, find λ
  3. Given v and λ, find f
  4. Converting between f and T
- **Real-world examples:** Radio waves, sound in different media, seismic waves

#### **Day 5: Lab 1 - Measuring Wave Properties**
- **Title:** "Determining Wave Speed on a String"
- **Materials:** String, mass set, pulley, vibration generator (or hand), timer
- **Procedure:** Vary tension, measure frequency and wavelength, calculate speed
- **Analysis:** Verify v = √(T/μ) for waves on strings

## **Week 2: Wave Behavior & Sound Waves**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Describe and explain reflection, refraction, diffraction, and interference
- Apply the principle of superposition
- Understand sound wave characteristics: pitch, loudness, quality
- Calculate speed of sound in different media

### **Daily Lesson Breakdown:**

#### **Day 6: Wave Behavior - Reflection & Refraction**
- **Reflection:** Law of reflection (angle in = angle out)
  - Demo: Ripple tank, echoes
- **Refraction:** Change in speed when entering new medium
  - Demo: Pencil in water, ripple tank with depth change
  - Snell's Law: n₁sinθ₁ = n₂sinθ₂ (introductory)

#### **Day 7: Wave Behavior - Diffraction & Interference**
- **Diffraction:** Bending around obstacles
  - Demo: Water waves through gap, sound around corners
  - Conditions for significant diffraction (λ ≈ gap size)
- **Interference:** Superposition principle
  - Constructive vs. destructive interference
  - Demo: Two speakers, ripple tank with two sources

#### **Day 8: Standing Waves & Resonance**
- **Formation:** Interference of two identical waves traveling in opposite directions
- **Nodes & Antinodes:** Positions of minimum and maximum displacement
- **Resonance:** Natural frequency matching driving frequency
  - Demo: Singing wine glass, swing set analogy
- **Applications:** Musical instruments, bridges (Tacoma Narrows case study)

#### **Day 9: Sound Waves - Characteristics**
- **Longitudinal pressure waves**
- **Characteristics:**
  - Pitch → Frequency (high f = high pitch)
  - Loudness → Amplitude (large A = loud sound)
  - Quality → Waveform (timbre)
- **Speed of sound:** v = √(B/ρ) (qualitative understanding)
  - Faster in solids > liquids > gases
  - Temperature dependence in air: v ≈ 331 + 0.6T m/s

#### **Day 10: Sound Applications & Doppler Effect**
- **Ultrasound:** Medical imaging, sonar
- **Infrasonic vs. ultrasonic**
- **Doppler Effect:** Frequency change due to relative motion
  - Formula: f' = f(v ± v₀)/(v ∓ vₛ)
  - Applications: Radar, astronomy (redshift), medical diagnostics
- **Worksheet:** Doppler effect calculations

## **Week 3: Light Waves & Electromagnetic Spectrum**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Describe electromagnetic waves and their properties
- Explain the electromagnetic spectrum and applications
- Understand polarization of light
- Apply wave concepts to light: reflection, refraction, diffraction

### **Daily Lesson Breakdown:**

#### **Day 11: Electromagnetic Waves**
- **Properties:** Transverse, can travel through vacuum, speed c = 3×10⁸ m/s
- **EM Spectrum:** Radio → Microwave → Infrared → Visible → UV → X-ray → Gamma
  - Frequency/wavelength ranges
  - Energy: E = hf (qualitative)
- **Activity:** "EM Spectrum Sorting" - match applications to wave type

#### **Day 12: Light Wave Behavior**
- **Reflection:** Mirrors (plane, concave, convex - qualitative)
- **Refraction:** Lenses (converging, diverging - qualitative)
  - Index of refraction: n = c/v
  - Snell's Law calculations
- **Total Internal Reflection:** Critical angle, fiber optics

#### **Day 13: Diffraction & Interference of Light**
- **Young's Double Slit Experiment:**
  - Formula: d sinθ = mλ (constructive), d sinθ = (m+½)λ (destructive)
  - Fringe spacing: Δy = λL/d
- **Single Slit Diffraction:** Qualitative understanding
- **Demo:** Laser through slits, diffraction grating

#### **Day 14: Polarization**
- **Concept:** Restriction of oscillation to one plane
- **Polarizers:** Malus' Law: I = I₀cos²θ
- **Applications:** Sunglasses, LCD screens, 3D movies
- **Activity:** Using polarizers to analyze light sources

#### **Day 15: Lab 2 - Measuring Wavelength of Light**
- **Title:** "Using a Diffraction Grating to Determine Wavelength"
- **Materials:** Laser, diffraction grating, screen, ruler
- **Procedure:** Measure fringe spacing, calculate λ
- **Analysis:** Compare to known laser wavelength, calculate error

## **Week 4: Advanced Concepts & Integration**

### **Learning Objectives:**
- Solve complex wave problems involving multiple concepts
- Apply wave principles to real-world technologies
- Prepare for examinations with integrated problem-solving
- Design and conduct wave-related experiments

### **Daily Lesson Breakdown:**

#### **Day 16: Complex Problem Solving**
- **Integrated Problems:**
  1. Sound wave interference with moving sources (Doppler + interference)
  2. Light refraction through multiple media
  3. Standing waves on strings with varying tension
  4. EM wave calculations (frequency ↔ wavelength ↔ energy)
- **Strategy:** Breaking down multi-step problems

#### **Day 17: Wave Technologies**
- **Communication:** Radio, TV, cell phones, WiFi
- **Medical:** Ultrasound imaging, MRI, X-rays
- **Scientific:** Seismology, astronomy (redshift), particle accelerators
- **Everyday:** Microwave ovens, remote controls, sunglasses
- **Research Activity:** Group presentations on specific technologies

#### **Day 18: Exam Preparation**
- **Common Exam Questions:**
  - Calculation problems (v = fλ, Snell's Law, interference)
  - Explanation questions (wave behaviors, applications)
  - Diagram interpretation (wave graphs, ray diagrams)
- **Past Paper Analysis:** Identify patterns and mark allocation
- **Study Skills:** Formula sheets, unit conversions, checking work

#### **Day 19: Project Work**
- **Options:**
  1. Build a musical instrument demonstrating standing waves
  2. Create educational video explaining a wave concept
  3. Design experiment to measure speed of sound
  4. Research paper on historical development of wave theory
- **In-class work time** with teacher guidance

#### **Day 20: Presentations & Review**
- **Project Presentations** (3-5 minutes each)
- **Unit Review Game:** "Wave Jeopardy" or quiz competition
- **Q&A Session:** Address remaining questions
- **Preview:** Next unit (Modern Physics: Quantum, Nuclear)

## **Sample Lesson: Day 3 - Wave Properties**

### **Lesson Objective:** Students will define and calculate wave properties.

### **Starter (5 min):**
- Show wave animation, ask: "What measurements could we take?"

### **Direct Instruction (15 min):**
- Define amplitude, wavelength, frequency, period, speed
- Show diagrams with labels
- Introduce v = fλ, T = 1/f

### **Guided Practice (15 min):**
- Work through examples on board:
  1. Wave with f = 10 Hz, λ = 2 m → v = ?
  2. Sound wave v = 340 m/s, f = 680 Hz → λ = ?
  3. Light wave λ = 500 nm → f = ? (unit conversion practice)

### **Independent Practice (10 min):**
- Worksheet with 5 calculation problems
- Peer checking of answers

### **Plenary (5 min):**
- Exit ticket: "If frequency doubles and speed stays constant, what happens to wavelength?"

### **Homework:**
- Textbook problems on wave calculations
