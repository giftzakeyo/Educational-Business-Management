## **🎯 Learning Objectives**

Students will be able to:
1. **State and explain** Newton's Three Laws of Motion
2. **Apply F=ma** to solve physics problems
3. **Identify action-reaction** force pairs
4. **Analyze real-world situations** using Newton's Laws
5. **Design experiments** to verify physical principles

## **💡 Teaching Tips**

### **Key Concepts to Emphasize:**
1. **First Law:** "Unless acted upon by unbalanced force"
2. **Second Law:** F=ma as quantitative relationship
3. **Third Law:** Forces act on DIFFERENT objects

### **Common Misconceptions to Address:**
- "Objects naturally come to rest" (Aristotle vs. Newton)
- "Action-reaction forces cancel" (they don't - different objects!)
- "Mass and weight are the same" (mass = matter, weight = force)

### **Engagement Strategies:**
- Start with surprising demonstrations
- Connect to student experiences (sports, vehicles)
- Use think-pair-share for difficult concepts
- Incorporate technology (simulations, videos)

### **Assessment Strategies:**
- Use exit tickets for quick checks
- Include real-world application questions
- Offer multiple ways to demonstrate understanding
- Provide timely, specific feedback
