## Formula Reference:
**F = m × a**  
Where:  
F = Force (Newtons, N)  
m = Mass (kilograms, kg)  
a = Acceleration (meters per second squared, m/s²)

**Remember:** 1 N = 1 kg·m/s²

## Part 2: Real-World Applications

**Instructions:** Solve these practical problems.

4. **Car Acceleration:**  
   A 1200 kg car accelerates from 0 to 60 km/h (16.67 m/s) in 8 seconds.  
   a) Calculate acceleration: a = (final velocity - initial velocity) ÷ time  
      a = (______ - 0) ÷ ______ = ______ m/s²  
   b) Calculate force: F = m × a = ______ × ______ = ______ N

5. **Rocket Launch:**  
   A small rocket with mass 0.5 kg produces 50 N of thrust.  
   a) Calculate acceleration: a = F ÷ m = ______ ÷ ______ = ______ m/s²  
   b) How does this compare to Earth's gravity (9.8 m/s²)?  
      Acceleration is ______ times gravity.

6. **Shopping Cart:**  
   You push a 15 kg shopping cart with 30 N of force.  
   a) Calculate acceleration: a = ______ ÷ ______ = ______ m/s²  
   b) If friction provides 10 N of resistance, what is the net force?  
      Net F = Applied F - Friction = ______ - ______ = ______ N  
   c) What is the actual acceleration with friction?  
      a = Net F ÷ m = ______ ÷ ______ = ______ m/s²
